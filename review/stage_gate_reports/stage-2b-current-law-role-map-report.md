# Stage 2B Current Law Role Map Report

Status: simulation stage-gate report. This is not legal advice, officer approval, statutory confirmation, financial sign-off or professional assurance.

## Scope

Stage 2B uses the sources acquired by Stage 2A to map current legal/governance roles for:

- Bristol as possible WPL licensing authority, order-maker and submitter;
- WECA/MCA as possible strategic, assurance, funding, consent, transferred-function or no-role body;
- DfT engagement versus Secretary of State confirmation.

## Gate Findings

- The statutory confirmation route has been narrowed: for an initial England-only WPL licensing scheme, Secretary of State confirmation is the controlled statutory assumption.
- DfT engagement is not confirmation. It can only evidence informal engagement, policy expectations, procedural expectations or formal confirmation if a source proves that status.
- The RPI-only exemption in the 2009 Regulations does not apply to an initial Bristol WPL order.
- Bristol promoter/order-maker/submission route remains unresolved because the acquired constitution and delegation sources do not expressly map the WPL order-making function.
- WECA/MCA current-law role remains unresolved because the acquired sources do not prove approval, consent, support, objection, transferred function or no-role status.

## Gate Decision

Stage 2B decision: **simulation sign-off with conditions for role-map use; simulation no-go remains for preferred scheme design, statutory consultation launch, OBC recommendation, FBC readiness, operational readiness and statutory submission readiness.**

## Register Effects

- `RISK-0003` is reduced from P0 to controlled P1 because the DfT/Secretary of State distinction is now source-bounded.
- `RISK-0001`, `RISK-0002`, `ISS-0001` and `ISS-0002` remain open.
- `EG-0021` is partially closed for confirmation-route classification but remains open for current DfT procedural expectations.
