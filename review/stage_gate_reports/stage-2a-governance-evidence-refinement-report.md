# Stage 2A Governance Evidence Refinement Report

Status: simulation gate note. This is not legal advice, officer approval, statutory confirmation, financial sign-off or professional assurance.

## Scope

This note records targeted evidence acquisition after the Stage 2 legal/governance baseline. It covers Bristol constitution/delegation sources, May 2025 WPL decision records, WECA/MCA constitution sources and current-law source controls.

## Evidence Added

- Bristol constitution/delegation sources: `SRC-BCC-0028` to `SRC-BCC-0034`.
- Bristol May 2025 WPL decision records: `SRC-BCC-0035`, `SRC-BCC-0036`.
- WECA/MCA constitution sources: `SRC-WECA-0011` to `SRC-WECA-0013`.
- WECA/MCA assurance, transport and committee-route sources: `SRC-WECA-0007`, `SRC-WECA-0014` to `SRC-WECA-0016`.
- Direct WPL statutory sources: `SRC-LEG-0017` to `SRC-LEG-0020`.
- Current-law WECA/MCA mapping sources: `SRC-LEG-0014`, `SRC-LEG-0016`, `SRC-LEG-0022`, `SRC-LEG-0023`.
- Negative-control current-law source: `SRC-LEG-0015`, which concerns charges for works in maintainable highways and must not be used as WPL authority.

## Findings

- The May 2025 WPL decision-record gap is closed. The decision notice and minutes classify the WPL item as an information update that committee noted; no implementation, consultation, OBC/FBC, preferred-scheme, boundary, charge, threshold, exemption or order-submission approval is evidenced.
- Bristol constitution sources support mapping of Transport and Connectivity Committee remit, key-decision procedure, Full Council reserved functions, access-to-information controls and officer delegation machinery.
- The acquired Bristol constitution sources do not expressly identify the WPL licensing scheme order-maker or the body/officer authorised to submit an initial order for confirmation.
- WECA/MCA constitution and current-law sources are acquired for mapping. They do not evidence formal WECA/MCA approval, support, objection, consent, funding decision, transferred WPL function or no-role status.
- Transport Act 2000 direct WPL sections strengthen the route map by identifying local and joint licensing scheme controls, but they still require mapping to the correct current non-metropolitan local traffic authority/licensing authority.
- Current revised WECA Order article pages confirm Part 3 transport omission notes after SI 2026/519. That narrows the current-law question but does not by itself settle the WPL role.

## Gate Decision

Stage 2A evidence refinement: **source acquisition and classification improved; simulation no-go remains for preferred scheme design, statutory consultation preparation, OBC recommendation, FBC readiness, operational readiness and statutory submission readiness.**
