# Stage 1 Spatial, Data, Operations And Enforcement Review

Status: simulated due-diligence review. This is not legal, statutory, data-protection, financial, operational or professional sign-off.

## Decision

Simulation no-go for Stage 4 spatial/options appraisal and operational readiness.

The statutory source base is stronger after acquiring `SRC-LEG-0011`, `SRC-LEG-0012` and `SRC-LEG-0013`, and the repo now has upgraded data schemas. That does not create a boundary, parking base, DPIA, licensing system, enforcement workflow or operating model.

- `SRC-LEG-0011`
- `SRC-LEG-0012`
- `SRC-LEG-0013`
- `schemas/parking-inventory.schema.json`

- `schemas/spatial-metadata.schema.json`

## Findings

- No selected boundary or authoritative boundary option dataset exists.
- No authoritative workplace parking inventory exists.
- No lawful-basis, DPIA or data-flow control exists for employer premises, inspection, enforcement or possible personal data.
- `SRC-LEG-0011` requires operational design for licence-charge liability, non-occupier parking providers, unlicensed parking, excess licensed places, licence-condition breach, PCN content, representations, rejection, county court appeals, charge certificates, recovery and notice service.
- `SRC-LEG-0012` reinforces accruals, expenses, interest, capital financing and proper-practices accounting controls for net proceeds.
- `SRC-LEG-0013` requires any vehicle-class differentiation to use statutory classes.
- Upgraded schemas give a starting contract for boundary, parking inventory, spatial metadata, licence records and enforcement events. They do not replace data acquisition or operational procedure design.

## Conditions

- Acquire authoritative spatial boundary and parking-base inputs with licences and metadata.
- Produce data-flow map, lawful-basis analysis and DPIA scope.
- Populate parking inventory and boundary option records against the schemas.
- Produce PCN templates, notice service policy, representation workflow, appeal/recovery procedure and evidence standards.
- Link operating procedures to finance, customer contact, cyber/security and accessibility controls.
