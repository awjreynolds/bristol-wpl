Status: simulated legal and consultation review. This is not legal advice, DfT advice, Bristol officer approval, statutory confirmation or professional assurance.

## Review Decision

Simulation sign-off with conditions for order-change terminology and no-overclaim controls. Simulation no-go remains for process readiness and route closure.

## Findings

| Finding | Severity | Disposition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Section 183 supports making, varying and revoking a licensing scheme by order. | P1 controlled | Add order-change terminology controls. |
| Section 184 supports confirmation for initial orders and ordinary variations, subject to specific exemptions. | P1 controlled | Maintain Secretary of State confirmation assumption for initial England-only order and ordinary variation. |
| The 2009 Regulations contain only a narrow RPI-linked licence-charge variation exemption. | P1 controlled | Prohibit extension to initial orders, non-RPI variations and revocation. |
| Section 184 expressly mentions revocation confirmation for joint local-London schemes, not on its face for Bristol-only schemes. | P1 open | Do not assert either that Bristol-only revocation requires confirmation or never requires confirmation. |
| Section 185 consultation/inquiry wording is permissive, but public-law consultation remains fact-sensitive. | P1 open | Keep consultation compliance controls open. |
| Publication, objections, notice and order-effect procedure remain insufficiently mapped. | P1 open | Keep `EG-0022` partially closed only. |
| Bristol final order-maker, variation-maker, revocation-maker, submitter and signatory route remains unresolved. | P0 inherited | Preserve Stage 2I no-go. |
