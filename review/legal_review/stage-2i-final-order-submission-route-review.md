# Stage 2I Final Order And Submission Route Review

Status: simulated legal, governance and Monitoring Officer review. This is not legal advice, Monitoring Officer advice, Bristol officer approval, statutory confirmation or professional assurance.

## Review Decision

Simulation sign-off with conditions for Stage 2I decision-box and route-option controls. Simulation no-go remains for route closure.

## Findings

| Finding | Severity | Disposition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Local evidence does not expressly identify the final Bristol decision body for making or approving the initial WPL licensing scheme order. | P0 | Keep `RISK-0001` and `ISS-0001` open. |
| Local evidence does not expressly identify the statutory submitter or signatory for Secretary of State confirmation. | P0 | Keep `EG-0020` open. |
| Monitoring Officer authentication power can execute a valid legal procedure document but does not itself identify the order-making decision body. | P1 controlled | Separate decision authority from document signing/submission. |
| Officer delegation is insufficient on current evidence for officer-only final order-making. | P0/P1 | Prohibit officer-only final order-making unless a future express source supports it. |
| Full Council is not proved mandatory, but it also cannot be ruled out generically. | P1 controlled | Require a Full Council trigger check in every final order/submission report. |
| The safest simulation option is a Full Council route if any budget/policy-framework, local-legislation, law-reserved or constitutional-interpretation trigger remains arguable. | P1 controlled | Use as conservative placeholder only, not as current approval. |

## Monitoring Officer Simulation Criteria

A simulated Monitoring Officer may close the route only if a future report records:

1. the express source-supported internal decision-maker for making or approving the final initial WPL licensing scheme order;
2. separate express authority to submit the order and statutory dossier to the Secretary of State, naming the submitting officer or signatory;
3. Full Council trigger analysis covering budget/policy framework, Local Transport Plan implications, licensing policy, local legislation and functions reserved by law;
4. separation between decision authority and document authentication;
5. key-decision controls: Forward Plan, notice, report, background papers and reasons;
6. resolution wording that distinguishes OBC, consultation launch, FBC, final order-making, submission and Secretary of State confirmation;
7. disclosure of open P0 dependencies, including WECA/MCA role and funding/assurance dependency.
