# Stage 1 Legal And Statutory Powers Review

Status: simulated due-diligence review. This is not legal advice, statutory confirmation, officer approval or professional sign-off.

## Decision

Simulation sign-off with conditions.

Stage 1 OBC legal-route scoping may continue on the explicit working assumption that a Bristol WPL would be an initial Transport Act 2000 licensing scheme order requiring Secretary of State confirmation before implementation. This is not sufficient for Stage 2 consultation, preferred-scheme approval, FBC readiness or statutory submission.

- `SRC-DFT-0001`
- `SRC-HMT-0001`
- Selected Bristol and WECA extracts including `SRC-BCC-0003`, `SRC-BCC-0004`, `SRC-BCC-0006`, `SRC-BCC-0008`, `SRC-BCC-0009`, `SRC-BCC-0014`, `SRC-WECA-0001`, `SRC-WECA-0003`, `SRC-WECA-0004`, `SRC-WECA-0006`

## Findings

- The apparent statutory spine is Transport Act 2000 Part III Chapter II.
- The evidence supports treating WPL as a licensing scheme, not a traffic regulation order.
- Section 184 supports the confirmation-control proposition. The England route points to Secretary of State confirmation, not mere DfT engagement.
- The 2009 Regulations RPI-only exemption is narrow: it applies only to a variation order whose sole purpose is altering licence charges in line with RPI. It does not exempt an initial Bristol WPL order.
- Schedule 12 and the 2003 Net Proceeds Regulations require the financial case to manage net proceeds, accounts, the opening ten-year general plan and opening five-year detailed programme.
- The 2001 Classes Regulations require any vehicle-class differentiation to use statutory classes and avoid incompatible mass/axle-class approaches.
- The authority route remains unresolved: Bristol competence/delegations, WECA/MCA role, order-maker and order-submitter are P0 controls.

## Conditions

- Resolve Bristol City Council competence and delegation route.
- Produce a current WECA/MCA function and constitution map.
- Keep DfT engagement separate from Secretary of State confirmation.
- Build the statutory order contents matrix covering area, times, charges, duration, thresholds, exemptions, vehicle classes, licences, enforcement, appeals, variation, revocation and review.
- Prepare the Schedule 12 general plan, detailed programme and accounting policy before any statutory submission simulation.
