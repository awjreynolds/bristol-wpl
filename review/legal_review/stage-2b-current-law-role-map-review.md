# Stage 2B Current Law Role Map Review

Status: simulated legal and governance review. This is not legal advice, statutory confirmation, officer approval or professional assurance.

## Review Decision

Simulation sign-off with conditions for the Stage 2B role map.

The role map is acceptable for controlling future drafting because it distinguishes:

- Transport Act WPL licensing scheme powers from ordinary transport-policy narrative;
- Secretary of State confirmation from informal DfT engagement;
- Bristol transport-governance machinery from a settled WPL order-making/submission route;
- WECA/MCA assurance and policy context from a settled statutory consent or no-role conclusion.

## Findings

| Finding | Severity | Disposition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The initial England WPL confirmation route is now source-bounded. Section 184 and section 198 support treating Secretary of State confirmation as the formal statutory control. | P1 controlled | `RISK-0003` may be reduced from P0 to controlled P1. |
| Bristol has transport committee and officer-delegation machinery, but no acquired source expressly names the WPL order-making or submission function. | P0 | `RISK-0001` and `ISS-0001` remain open. |
| WECA/MCA sources are adequate to prevent overclaiming but inadequate to conclude approval, consent, transferred function or no-role status. | P0 | `RISK-0002` and `ISS-0002` remain open. |
| The 2009 RPI-only exemption must remain a prohibition against route-shortening for an initial order. | P1 controlled | Keep as legal compliance matrix control. |

## Conditions

- Future legal/business-case text must cite the Stage 2B map before making any role, promoter or confirmation-route claim.
- Draft OBC/FBC material must continue to use "working assumption" language for Bristol promoter status until the legal source note is completed.
- WECA/MCA must remain "context or possible assurance/funding dependency" unless a source-specific role is found.
- Statutory submission readiness remains blocked.

## Simulation Sign-Off

The Stage 2B role map is signed off for evidence control and issue narrowing only. It does not sign off scheme legality, consultation readiness, OBC/FBC readiness, operations readiness or statutory submission readiness.
