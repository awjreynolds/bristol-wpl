# Stage 3A Strategic Assessment Review

Status: simulation peer review.  
Date: 2026-06-26.  
Scope: Stage 3A strategic assessment, objective, theory-of-change, benefit, dependency and investment-programme control architecture.

This review records simulated agent due diligence only. It is not Bristol officer approval, legal advice, financial certification, transport-economics advice or professional assurance for real-world use.

## Review Criteria

The Stage 3A package was reviewed against these criteria:

- strategic assessment controls must not select a preferred WPL scheme, shortlist, charge design, exemption package, consultation proposition or investment package;
- problem statements must distinguish observed Bristol evidence, comparator evidence, policy aspiration, assumption, inference and gap;
- objectives and CSFs must be outcome-based and intervention-neutral;
- theory of change must separate WPL-only, transport-investment-only and combined-package causal pathways;
- stakeholders and affected places must be identified without inventing consultation responses, support or opposition;
- WECA/MCA strategy, funding and assurance context must not be converted into approval, consent, support, objection, funding decision or no-role status;
- benefits must not be quantified or used for BCR, VFM, mode-shift, congestion, distributional or investment claims;
- package and cost-line funding assurance controls must preserve Stage 2H trigger rules.

## Strategic Case Review Findings

The Strategic Case Review Agent found that Stage 3A can proceed only as control architecture.

Required controls are now present for:

- problem definition and evidence-basis tagging;
- baseline and trend evidence-needs recording;
- intervention-neutral objective and CSF rules;
- theory-of-change separation and failure mechanisms;
- benefits control and attribution route classification;
- dependency and constraint visibility;
- package maturity and funding-assurance classification.

Finding: the package is suitable as a strategic assessment control layer if it remains expressly non-decision-grade.

Limitation: no completed problem definition, baseline, objective set, stakeholder evidence, preferred way forward, investment package or Strategic Case exists.

## Funding Assurance and Benefits Red-Team Findings

The Funding Assurance and Benefits Red-Team Agent found four main risk areas:

| Risk area | Stage 3A control |
|---|---|
| Treating Stage 2H as WECA/MCA funding approval | Package funding classification preserves formal decision-source and assurance-route requirements. |
| Treating header-only benefits as claimable benefits | Benefits registers remain control placeholders and require baseline, target, attribution, uncertainty and reviewer status. |
| Using gross levy receipts as economic benefits | Benefits register and benefits map classify receipts as transfer/public cash-flow only. |
| Treating candidate packages as mature | Package registers require sponsor, scope, cost, funding, OAR/ASR/ASST, assurance route, benefits and gate effect before reliance. |

Finding: Stage 3A addresses the main false-readiness risks for a strategic control stage.

Limitation: every future package or cost line still needs funding source, MCA trigger, OAR/ASR/ASST route, reviewer and gate effect before reliance.
