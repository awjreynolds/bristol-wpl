## Findings

- WECA/MCA strategic documents do not amount to approval of a Bristol WPL.
- JLTP4 supports considering demand management and WPL-type mechanisms but does not approve a Bristol scheme.
- Bus Strategy and BSIP material treat WPL/demand management as policy or funding levers, not as Bristol scheme approval.
- `SRC-WECA-0006` is relevant where funding or assurance interfaces arise, but it does not prove WPL licensing approval or statutory consent.
- Current-law WECA/MCA role remains unresolved. The 2017 Order and 2026 amendment source material trigger further legal mapping rather than settling the answer.

## Conditions

- Confirm Bristol licensing authority competence and delegation route.
- Confirm whether current devolution/WECA/MCA law creates transferred, concurrent, consent, assurance or no direct WPL role.
- Complete WECA/MCA constitution, delegation and meeting-record checks.
- Resolve any WECA/MCA funding or assurance dependency for the net-proceeds transport package.
