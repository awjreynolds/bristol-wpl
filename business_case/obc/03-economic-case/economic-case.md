## Appraisal Framework

**Proposal.** The economic case should be prepared under the Green Book, DfT transport business case guidance and TAG, with a documented Options Assessment Report, Appraisal Specification Report, appraisal summary tables, model cards and uncertainty log. The WECA FBC exemplar shows the expected pattern: define appraisal methodology, demand forecast approach, scheme costs, economic appraisal summary, value-for-money statement, sensitivity testing and unquantified impacts (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 56-64 and 1126-1157).

**Gap.** The repo does not hold an agreed ASR, model outputs, monetised benefit calculations, BCR, VFM category or decision-grade sensitivity tests. This section cannot claim economic readiness.

User charges and transfers must be separated from real resource costs and benefits. Gross WPL receipts are not economic benefits simply because they are revenue.

## Demand, Behaviour And Transport Effects

**Cited.** Bristol's March 2026 update records that parking data would feed into modelling, forecasting and impact assessment to predict revenue and assess likely impacts on congestion, air quality and economic outcomes (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4340-4343). It also records low initial survey response and supplementary data collection (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4318-4329).

**Gap.** No validated workplace parking base, employer behaviour model, elasticity assumption, mode-shift estimate, trip-retiming estimate, relocation/displacement model, CPZ/RPZ response, congestion forecast, air-quality forecast or public-transport impact estimate is held in this repo.

**Requirement.** Future modelling must show input data, source quality, assumptions, uncertainty ranges, calibration, sensitivity testing and reviewer sign-off. Any Nottingham-based parameter must be treated as comparator evidence only until Bristol transferability is evidenced.

## Value For Money

**No decision-grade VFM.** This simulation does not calculate a BCR or assign a VFM category. The WECA FBC exemplar demonstrates how a VFM section can be presented when methodology, costs, benefits and sensitivity tests exist (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 1126-1157 and 1389-1413), but those inputs are not held for Bristol WPL.

The eventual VFM assessment should include:

- appraisal period and price base;
- optimism bias and risk adjustment;
- core and low/high demand cases;
- sensitivity to boundary, charge, exemptions, compliance and displacement;
- treatment of revenue as transfer and net proceeds as financial resource;
- non-monetised equalities, business, land-use, public-realm, accessibility and environmental impacts;
- switching values for key assumptions.

Until this exists, no BCR, VFM category or benefits-readiness claim is permitted.
