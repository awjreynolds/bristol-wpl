## Commercial Strategy

**Proposal.** A Bristol WPL commercial strategy would need to define the services required to create, launch and operate a licensing scheme. The WECA FBC exemplar includes output specification, procurement strategy, sourcing options, payment mechanisms, risk allocation, contract length and contract management (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 1619-1645). The WPL equivalent should cover:

- employer account registration and annual licensing;
- workplace parking place declaration and verification;
- billing, payment, debt management and refunds;
- exemptions, discounts and change-of-circumstance processing;
- inspection, enforcement, representations and appeals support;
- digital service, CRM, document management and customer contact;
- data protection, cyber security, audit logging and retention;
- integration with finance, GIS, parking inventory and statutory reporting.

**Gap.** No operating model, procurement strategy, service design, system architecture, cyber assessment or supplier-market analysis is complete.

## Commercial Risks

| Risk | Why it matters | Control required |
|---|---|---|
| Supplier-market weakness | Specialist WPL systems are uncommon in England | Market engagement and procurement strategy |
| Data-quality failure | Employer liability depends on accurate parking declarations and inventory | Data model, audit trail and inspection protocol |
| Enforcement and appeals volume | Costs and public trust could be materially affected | Enforcement policy and appeal workflow |
| Cyber and privacy failure | Employer and contact data require robust protection | DPIA, cyber review and processor controls |
| Public-facing service failure | Licensing and payment errors could harm legitimacy | User testing, accessibility review and support model |
| Procurement before legal certainty | Could create sunk cost or abortive work | Stage-gated procurement authority and break clauses |
