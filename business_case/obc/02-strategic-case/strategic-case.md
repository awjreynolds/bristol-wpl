## Case For Change

**Cited.** Bristol's March 2026 WPL update describes the proposed WPL as part of the city's transport strategy to reduce congestion and generate revenue for transport improvements, with aims to discourage car commuting, promote walking, cycling and public transport, and improve health, environment and land-use efficiency (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4285-4288). It also records that parking data was being gathered from workplace surveys, door-knocking, telephone surveys and off-street parking audits to support modelling and impact assessment (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4310-4338).

**Controlled inference.** The strategic case for considering a WPL is therefore not just revenue generation. It is a demand-management, mode-shift and transport-investment proposition that would need to demonstrate proportionality, distributional fairness, deliverability and reinvestment in local transport policies.

**Gap.** The repo does not yet hold the final Bristol problem statement, selected WPL package, spatial boundary, employer parking base, displacement analysis or consultation-tested proposition. This section cannot be used to justify a preferred option.

## Policy Fit

**Cited.** The Bristol WPL update states that a WPL is referenced in and consulted on as part of Joint Local Transport Plan 4 and the Bristol Transport Strategy (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4468-4469). WECA/MCA strategy sources such as JLTP4, the Bus Strategy and BSIP are held as strategic context. The WECA FBC exemplar shows that WECA-facing business cases normally locate a scheme within Combined Authority and Bristol policy context before turning to scheme-specific evidence (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 193-213).

**WECA/MCA tag: `strategic_context`.** WECA/MCA policy material can support context and alignment language only. It must not be used to state that WECA/MCA has approved, funded, consented to, sponsored, objected to or declined a role in Bristol WPL.

**Statutory context.** The March 2026 Bristol update states that WPL powers are contained in the Transport Act 2000, that employers would need licences for liable workplace parking places, that net revenue must be reinvested after operating costs, and that Secretary of State approval is required before implementation (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4351-4361). This is a useful public-source summary, but the legal route remains controlled by the statutory dossier and legal compliance matrix.
