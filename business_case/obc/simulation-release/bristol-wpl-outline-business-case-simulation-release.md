## 1. Executive Summary

**Cited.** Bristol's March 2026 WPL update records that the OBC programme was in progress, with the scheme-development and final-OBC work planned for spring 2026 (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4276-4295 and 4306-4308). It also records that modelling, forecasting and impact assessment would be used to test revenue, congestion, air quality and economic outcomes (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4340-4343).

**Gap.** This repo does not hold a completed or approved Bristol WPL OBC. It does not hold a selected boundary, charge, threshold, exemption package, operating-hours proposal, BCR, VFM category, consultation launch authority, FBC, scheme order or statutory submission.

**Inference.** The simulation can produce a professional OBC-shaped working draft, but the correct gate status remains no-go for reliance because `ISS-0011`, `RISK-0014` and the Stage 7 OBC gate remain blocked in the control trail.

**Inference.** WECA/MCA sources are used with trigger tags `strategic_context` and `conditional_funding_assurance_interface` only, as controlled in `analysis/weca-obc-fbc-exemplars/comparator-matrix.csv`, `governance/issues_register.csv` row `ISS-0002` and `governance/risk_register.csv` row `RISK-0002`. ## 2. Strategic Case

**Cited.** Bristol's public WPL framing identifies demand management and transport reinvestment objectives: reducing congestion, generating revenue for transport improvements, discouraging car commuting, promoting walking, cycling and public transport, and improving health, environment and land-use efficiency (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4285-4288).

**Cited.** The same source states that WPL powers sit in the Transport Act 2000, that employers would need licences for liable workplace parking places, that net revenue must be reinvested into local transport after operating costs, and that Secretary of State approval is required before implementation (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4351-4361).

**Proposal.** The strategic objectives for future review should cover reduced avoidable workplace car commuting, legally compliant local transport reinvestment, improved sustainable transport options, managed displacement/equality impacts and an enforceable licensing service.

**Gap.** No final Bristol problem statement, selected option, boundary, investment package, displacement mitigation or consultation-tested proposition is held. Any future strategic case must keep Bristol evidence, WECA/MCA context and Nottingham comparator lessons separate.

## 3. Economic Case

**Proposal.** The economic case should follow Green Book, DfT transport business case and TAG discipline. It should include an OAR, ASR, ASST, model cards, appraisal tables, uncertainty analysis and reviewer sign-off before any BCR or VFM language is used.

**Cited comparator pattern.** The WECA FBC exemplar defines appraisal methodology, demand forecast approach, economic appraisal summary, unquantified impacts and VFM assessment as part of the Economic Case (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 1126-1157).

**Gap.** Bristol WPL has no repo-held agreed ASR, model output, BCR, VFM category, demand response, revenue model, boundary displacement model, CPZ/RPZ assessment or quantified benefits. Gross WPL revenue must not be treated as an economic benefit without proper appraisal treatment.

## 4. Financial Case

**Cited.** Current Bristol evidence supports only the OBC-development budget: GBP1m funded through the Clean Air Zone Fund (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4506-4513).

**Proposal.** A future Financial Case should set out price base, chargeable-space base, exemptions, compliance, operating costs, implementation costs, gross income, net proceeds, borrowing/grant interface, reserve/cashflow treatment and sensitivity tests.

**Proposal.** If any WPL-funded package uses WECA/MCA-administered funding or requires Combined Authority programme approval, the package and cost-line funding/assurance classification must be completed before reliance. This is controlled as `conditional_funding_assurance_interface` in `analysis/weca-obc-fbc-exemplars/comparator-matrix.csv`, with unresolved WECA/MCA role and funding-interface blockers recorded at `ISS-0002`, `RISK-0002` and `EG-0060`.

**Gap.** No Section 151 certification, affordability approval, WECA/MCA funding approval, net-proceeds plan or implementation cost model exists in this repo.

## 5. Commercial Case

### 9.2 Business Need

**Cited.** Bristol public WPL evidence frames the scheme as demand management and transport reinvestment, with objectives linked to congestion, commuting behaviour and sustainable transport (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4285-4288).

**Cited.** The March 2026 record also says the OBC work would test revenue, congestion, air quality, equality, economic impacts and other outcomes (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4340-4343).

**Inference.** A future Bristol WPL OBC therefore needs to demonstrate more than technical charging feasibility. It must show why the intervention is needed, why a WPL is proportionate, how it interacts with the transport strategy, how effects are distributed, and how the licensing scheme would be delivered.

**Gap.** The repo has no final problem baseline, no agreed theory of change, no quantified counterfactual and no consultation-tested business need.

## 10. Options And Spatial Definition

### 10.1 Longlist Framework

**Proposal.** A future OBC should start from a longlist that includes:

- do minimum: existing transport and parking policies without a WPL;
- non-WPL demand-management measures;
- parking-management measures outside a WPL licensing scheme;
- WPL city-wide variants;
- WPL area-based variants;
- WPL variants with different thresholds, exemptions, charges and operating hours;
- phased implementation variants;
- alternative transport-investment package variants.

**Cited comparator pattern.** The WECA SOC comparator records options and work packages at early maturity and uses that material to inform later OBC development (`SRC-WECA-0008` lines 538-565).

**Gap.** No Bristol WPL longlist, shortlist, sift criteria, spatial option set or preferred-way-forward is held.

### 10.2 Shortlist And Sift Criteria

| Criterion | Why it matters for WPL | Evidence needed |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic fit | WPL must address a transport problem, not just raise money | Problem baseline, objectives, policy alignment |
| Legal deliverability | The licensing scheme must be within statutory powers and process | Legal compliance matrix and decision route |
| Spatial coherence | Boundary must be administrable, enforceable and fair | GIS boundary, parking inventory, edge-effect analysis |
| Economic performance | Costs, benefits, transfers and uncertainty need robust appraisal | OAR, ASR, ASST, models and sensitivity analysis |
| Financial affordability | Net proceeds and costs need credible accounting | Finance model, S151 review, cashflow |
| Commercial deliverability | Systems and services must be procurable and operable | Market, procurement and operating model evidence |
| Public acceptability | Employers, workers and residents must be heard | Consultation plan, EqIA, stakeholder evidence |
| Displacement risk | Parking may move into residential or uncontrolled streets | Baseline parking surveys and mitigation options |

**Gap.** The current repository does not contain the datasets or review outcomes needed to score these criteria.

### 10.3 Boundary And Parking Inventory

**Proposal.** The spatial case should include an editable boundary schedule and GIS package with:

- charging-area polygons;
- edge streets and split-site rules;
- workplace site references;
- liable and exempt parking-place classification;
- employer/site survey evidence;
- controlled-parking, residents-parking and unrestricted-street baseline;
- enforcement geography and inspection routes;
- equality and accessibility sensitive locations;
- public transport, walking and cycling accessibility layers;
- audit trail from raw sources to final inventory.

**Gap.** No authoritative boundary or workplace parking inventory exists. Stage 4A controls define what is needed through `analysis/spatial/stage-4a-boundary-and-parking-inventory-control-package.md`, `ISS-0003` and `RISK-0004`, but they do not create the data.

### 10.4 Nottingham Transferability

**Inference.** Nottingham remains the only implemented UK WPL precedent captured in the repo, but Stage 18A treats Nottingham as lessons and transferability questions only (`docs/officer/nottingham-and-comparator-lessons.md`, `governance/pitfalls_register.csv` row `PIT-0039`, `RISK-0006`, `RISK-0009`).

**Proposal.** The future Bristol OBC should include a specific displacement subsection covering employment areas, residential streets, CPZ/RPZ interaction, enforcement capacity and mitigation sequencing.

**Gap.** Nottingham's experience cannot be copied into Bristol forecasts. Bristol needs its own boundary, street baseline, employer segmentation, survey evidence and mitigation-cost evidence.

## 11. Licensing, Operations And Revenue Service

### 11.1 Licensing Scheme Design

**Cited.** Bristol public evidence states that employers would need a licence for liable workplace parking places and that charges may be levied for licensing workplace parking places (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4351-4358).

**Proposal.** The future OBC licensing design should define:

- liable workplace parking place;
- licence holder;
- application and renewal cycle;
- declaration and evidence requirements;
- change-of-circumstance route;
- exemptions and discounts;
- inspection powers and evidence handling;
- penalty and enforcement process;
- representations, appeals support and complaint route;
- payment, refunds and debt management;
- data retention, privacy and cyber controls;
- accessibility and assisted-digital arrangements.

**Gap.** The current repo does not hold a complete licensing policy, operating procedure, IT system design, privacy notice, DPIA or enforcement policy.

### 11.2 Revenue And Net Proceeds

**Cited.** Bristol public evidence states that all net revenue must be reinvested into local transport after operating costs (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4358-4361).

**Proposal.** A future Financial Case should separate:

- gross charge income;
- expected compliance and non-payment assumptions;
- operating costs;
- implementation costs;
- enforcement and appeals costs;
- reserves and bad-debt treatment;
- net proceeds;
- local transport programme allocation;
- borrowing or grant interactions;
- sensitivity to boundary, charge, exemptions and employer behaviour.

**Gap.** No chargeable-space inventory, charge, compliance forecast, operating-cost forecast, net-proceeds plan or fund-accounting treatment exists. No number in this simulation is decision-grade.

### 11.3 Commercial And Procurement Strategy

**Cited comparator pattern.** The WECA FBC exemplar covers output specification, procurement strategy, sourcing options, payment mechanisms, risk allocation, contract length and contract management (`SRC-WECA-0009` lines 1619-1645).

**Proposal.** The WPL Commercial Case should test at least three delivery models:

| Delivery model | Possible scope | Main control question |
|---|---|---|
| In-house with procured platform | Council retains service, buys licensing/payment/case-management tools | Can retained capability manage volume, compliance and cyber risk? |
| Managed service | Supplier operates platform and customer/admin processes under council control | How are discretion, enforcement, data and accountability protected? |
| Hybrid | Council retains policy/enforcement decisions, supplier provides platform and support | Which risks sit with the council and which transfer to supplier? |

**Gap.** No supplier-market evidence, procurement route, specification, contract model, value-for-money procurement analysis or retained-client capability assessment exists.
