## Financial Model Scope

**Cited current position.** Bristol's March 2026 report-pack finance advice records a GBP1m Clean Air Zone Fund allocation for WPL OBC-stage development work, covering supporting studies, surveys, modelling, scheme development and stakeholder engagement (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4506-4513). This is development-budget evidence only; it is not evidence that a completed Bristol WPL OBC has been approved.

**Gap.** That evidence does not provide an implementation budget, operating-cost model, chargeable-space forecast, revenue forecast, affordability statement, borrowing strategy, net-proceeds account, capital programme or Section 151 certification.

The future model scope must include:

- price base, inflation and indexation assumptions;
- boundary and workplace parking place base;
- liable employers and small-employer threshold;
- exemptions, discounts and transition rules;
- compliance and non-compliance assumptions;
- gross charge income and timing;
- operating costs, enforcement costs and appeals costs;
- implementation, mobilisation, systems and staff costs;
- net proceeds and restrictions on use;
- financing, cash flow, reserves, borrowing or grant interface;
- downside and switching-value cases.

## Revenue And Net Proceeds

**Cited statutory/public position.** Bristol's March 2026 update states that all net revenue raised must be reinvested into improving local transport after operating costs (`SRC-BCC-0015` lines 4351-4360). The statutory detail must be controlled through the legal compliance matrix and net-proceeds regulations sources.

**No forecast.** This simulation does not present gross charge income, operating expenditure, net proceeds or funding capacity. No number should be inserted until the parking base, boundary, charge, threshold, exemptions, compliance and cost assumptions are controlled.

**Requirement.** Every future revenue figure must cite a model run and assumptions table. Every future net-proceeds claim must cite the statutory dossier and finance review.

## Sensitivity And Risk

The future financial case should include sensitivity tests for:

- lower workplace parking base;
- lower compliance;
- lower employer pass-through to travel behaviour;
- delayed implementation;
- higher operating and enforcement costs;
- consultation-driven exemptions or discounts;
- lower or delayed transport-package spend;
- displaced parking requiring CPZ/RPZ mitigation;
- CAZ repayment or interaction with other transport funds;
- inflation and indexation assumptions.

Current status: no sensitivity analysis is held.
