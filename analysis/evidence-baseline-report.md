### Appraisal and Assurance Spine

The controlling appraisal structure is: SOC-equivalent strategic assessment / preferred way forward before OBC detailed appraisal, then OAR/ASR/ASST before detailed modelling, then OBC, consultation, FBC reappraisal and statutory dossier. No OBC/FBC recommendation may be drafted before the SOC-equivalent, options and ASR evidence is complete.

The evidence base has acquired current landing/control sources for Green Book, DfT transport business case, TAG and WECA assurance. Individual TAG units, detailed VfM/data-book inputs and source notes remain required before modelling reliance.

### Spatial, Parking Inventory and Data Readiness

The simulation has no selected WPL boundary and no authoritative boundary input package. Stage 4/options appraisal must not proceed until official boundary data, spatial licences, LLPG/UPRN or equivalent address/premises data, parking-base sources, lawful basis and privacy controls are defined.

Parking inventory, boundary, spatial metadata, licence record and enforcement event schemas now exist as data contracts. They do not create source data or operating readiness. XLSX registers are officer views only. Real personal, VRM, inspection or enforcement data must not be committed to normal Git paths.

### Dossier Architecture Readiness

The Stage 0 architecture is sufficient as a scaffold and Stage 1 now includes editable Markdown OBC/FBC/statutory templates, an expanded statutory crosswalk and an expanded legal compliance matrix. These are not operationally sufficient for OBC/FBC/statutory drafting. No OBC/FBC prose should be assembled until the legal route, strategic assessment, spatial/data baseline, modelling, ASR and source notes are established.

### Current Law / Statutory Route

Legal review identifies Transport Act 2000 Part III Chapter II as the apparent statutory spine for an initial Bristol WPL simulation, subject to Stage 2 legal review. Stage 2C source-chain work now supports treating Bristol City Council as the proposed Bristol local licensing authority/order-maker under investigation for simulation drafting, and Stage 2D supports Transport and Connectivity Committee as the best-evidenced member route for OBC/progression/FBC decisions. Final WPL licensing-scheme order-making and statutory submission route remains a P0 issue. An initial England-only scheme should be treated as requiring Secretary of State confirmation before coming into force. DfT engagement must remain separate from statutory confirmation. The RPI-only variation exemption in the 2009 Regulations must not be applied to an initial order, ordinary variation or revocation.

Stage 2 must resolve the P0 questions over Bristol internal decision route, WECA/MCA current role, who submits the order, and current DfT/Secretary of State expectations before any simulated preferred scheme or consultation reliance.
