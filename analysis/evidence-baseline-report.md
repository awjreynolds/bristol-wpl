## Discovery Handover Synthesis

Discovery agent outputs will be reconciled here by source ID and issue severity. Any claim not supported by a source ID, official URL, or explicit assumption will remain out of the baseline.

### Bristol Decision / Governance Trail

The seed metadata supports a chronology skeleton but not substantive decision content. `SRC-BCC-0004` is the only row explicitly classified as a decision notice. Committee reports, packs and updates must be treated as recommendations, evidence or status material until matched to decisions or minutes. Forward plans show planned decision timing only. EqIA rows show equality-assessment status only. Public forum and media rows are not decision evidence.

Priority acquisition: `SRC-BCC-0004`, then decision notices or minutes for the May 2025, October 2025 and March 2026 committee rows.

### Nottingham and UK Comparator Evidence

Comparator rows are useful leads, but all remain `seeded_not_downloaded`. No comparator impact, revenue, charge, mode-share or transferability claim is ready for OBC use. Nottingham official sources should be used only for Nottingham’s own scheme narrative and operational design after acquisition. Independent causal or quasi-causal claims should be limited to the academic/evaluation sources after method review.

Priority acquisition: Nottingham annual/current operational sources, independent congestion and mode-share studies, Leicester/TfL comparator material and latest official status evidence for other UK WPL proposals.

### WECA/MCA Role Evidence

Seed WECA/MCA material currently supports role hypotheses only. It may evidence adopted strategy context, assurance framework relevance, business-case process comparison or programme/data dependencies. It does not establish formal WECA/MCA approval, funding approval, Bristol WPL support, objection or statutory consent position.

The Stage 2 P0 question is whether current amended legislation, devolution instruments and constitutions give WECA/MCA transferred, concurrent, consent, assurance or no direct WPL role. Until verified, WECA/MCA support and power must not be asserted.

### Appraisal and Assurance Spine

The controlling appraisal structure is: SOC-equivalent strategic assessment / preferred way forward before OBC detailed appraisal, then OAR/ASR/ASST before detailed modelling, then OBC, consultation, FBC reappraisal and statutory dossier. No OBC/FBC recommendation may be drafted before the SOC-equivalent, options and ASR evidence is complete.

The evidence base must acquire current official guidance snapshots/source notes before relying on Green Book, DfT transport business case, TAG, DfT VfM or WECA assurance requirements.

### Spatial, Parking Inventory and Data Readiness

The simulation has no selected WPL boundary and no authoritative boundary input package. Stage 4/options appraisal must not proceed until official boundary data, spatial licences, LLPG/UPRN or equivalent address/premises data, parking-base sources, lawful basis and privacy controls are defined.

Parking inventory must use a normalised canonical data contract. XLSX registers are officer views only. Real personal, VRM, inspection or enforcement data must not be committed to normal Git paths.

### Dossier Architecture Readiness

The Stage 0 architecture is sufficient as a scaffold, but not operationally sufficient for OBC/FBC/statutory drafting. The statutory crosswalk and legal compliance matrix are header-only. The assembly scripts are placeholders. No OBC/FBC prose should be assembled until the legal route, compliance matrix, crosswalk, strategic assessment, spatial/data baseline and ASR are established.

## Initial Baseline Controls

- Formal decisions, officer recommendations, strategy context, consultation material and media coverage must remain separate.
- Absence of a WECA/MCA decision is not evidence of opposition.
- Strategic mention of WPL is not evidence of formal project support.
- Initial WPL scheme order, variation, revocation and RPI-only variation routes must remain separate.
- No preferred option can be asserted at evidence baseline stage.

### Current Law / Statutory Route

D1 discovery identifies Transport Act 2000 Part III Chapter II as the apparent statutory spine for an initial Bristol WPL simulation, subject to Stage 2 legal review. An initial England-only scheme appears to require Secretary of State confirmation before coming into force. DfT engagement must remain separate from statutory confirmation. The RPI-only variation exemption in the 2009 Regulations must not be applied to an initial order, ordinary variation or revocation.

Stage 2 must resolve the P0 questions over Bristol competence/delegations, WECA/MCA current role, who submits the order, and current DfT/Secretary of State expectations before any simulated preferred scheme or consultation reliance.
