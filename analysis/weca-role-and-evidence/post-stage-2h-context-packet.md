## Current Controlled Position

WECA/MCA is currently controlled as:

- strategic context where cited;
- conditional funding/assurance interface where a named funding stream, business-case assurance route, programme-entry route, CRSTS/TCR/LTDP/mass-transit dependency or programme-change control is triggered;
- not evidenced as a formal Bristol WPL approver, funder, sponsor, supporter, objector or consent body;
- not evidenced as having a transferred or concurrent WPL order-making function in the scoped current-law material;
- not cleared as having no role or no consent requirement.

## Permitted Wording

- WECA/MCA and West of England strategies are relevant strategic context for demand management, public transport funding and regional transport alignment.
- WECA/MCA assurance may become relevant if a Bristol WPL development cost or WPL-funded package uses, matches, changes, depends on or interfaces with MCA funding, programme assurance, delegated approvals or regional delivery arrangements.
- Stage 2G did not locate a formal WECA/MCA or Joint Committee decision approving, supporting, objecting to, consenting to, sponsoring or funding a Bristol Workplace Parking Levy within the bounded official public-record search run on 26 June 2026.
- Stage 2H classifies CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit, investment-fund and delivery-assurance records as conditional package-level funding/assurance interface evidence only.

## Prohibited Wording

- WECA/MCA approved, supports, objects to, funded, sponsored or consented to Bristol WPL.
- WECA/MCA consent is required.
- WECA/MCA consent is not required.
- WECA/MCA has no role.
- Bristol WPL is a WECA/MCA scheme.
- MCA assurance framework material is statutory WPL approval.
- Stage 2G proves no-role or no-consent.
- WPL net proceeds can be substituted into CRSTS, TCR, Investment Fund or mass-transit packages without a package-specific funding, grant, change-control and decision route.
