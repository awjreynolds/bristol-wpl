# Stage 2H Package Funding And Assurance Trigger Map

Status: simulated finance, legal and governance due-diligence artefact. This is not legal advice, funding approval, WECA/MCA approval, Bristol officer advice, statutory confirmation, assurance approval or professional finance advice.

## Purpose

Stage 2H classifies when West of England MCA/WECA funding, assurance or programme-change controls might become relevant to a Bristol Workplace Parking Levy business case or statutory dossier.

The control is deliberately narrow. It separates:

1. the Bristol WPL licensing scheme and its statutory approval route;
2. the source of funds for WPL development and implementation;
3. the funded transport package that WPL net proceeds may enable;
4. any WECA/MCA funding, assurance, CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit or investment-programme interface.

Stage 2H does not settle whether WECA/MCA has a statutory role, consent role, consultation-response role or no role in the WPL licensing scheme. It classifies package-level triggers so later OBC/FBC drafting does not turn strategic or assurance context into approval language.

## Controlled Finding

WECA/MCA CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit, investment-fund and delivery-assurance records are classified as **conditional package-level funding/assurance interface evidence only**.

They may be relevant if a Bristol WPL-funded transport package uses, matches, changes, depends on or interfaces with WECA/MCA funding, programme assurance, delegated approvals or regional delivery arrangements. They do not evidence WECA/MCA approval, support, objection, consent, sponsorship, funding approval, statutory confirmation, WPL order-making power, transferred WPL function or no-role conclusion for the Bristol WPL licensing scheme.

## Required Package-Level Classification

Every WPL development-cost line, implementation-cost line and transport package component must include a funding and assurance classification before OBC/FBC reliance:

| Field | Required value |
| --- | --- |
| Package or cost-line ID | Stable local ID. |
| Description | Plain English description of the package or cost line. |
| WPL relationship | `development_cost`, `implementation_cost`, `net_proceeds_package`, `match_funded_package`, `interface_only`, `unrelated`. |
| Funding sources | Bristol, WPL net proceeds, MCA/WECA stream, DfT stream, third-party, borrowing, other. |
| WECA/MCA trigger class | One or more classes from the taxonomy above. |
| Formal decision source | Source ID, meeting, decision-maker, date and decision effect, or `not evidenced`. |
| Assurance route | None, Bristol only, MCA assurance, DfT, TAG/OAR/ASR, other. |
| Statutory WPL effect | None evidenced, statutory role under review, consent under review, decision required. |
| Evidence status | Formal decision, adopted strategy, assurance framework, programme context, inference, assumption, gap. |
| Gate effect | P0, P1, P2 or non-blocking context. |
| Owner and reviewer | Named agent role and simulation reviewer. |

## Controlled Wording

Permitted:

- WECA/MCA CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit, investment-fund and delivery-assurance records are conditional package-level funding/assurance interface evidence only.
- WECA/MCA assurance may become relevant if a Bristol WPL development cost or WPL-funded transport package uses, matches, changes, depends on or interfaces with MCA funding, programme assurance, delegated approvals or regional delivery arrangements.
- Current repo evidence describes the Stage One/OBC development budget as Bristol Clean Air Zone Fund, not as a formal WECA/MCA WPL funding approval.
- Stage 2G did not locate a formal WECA/MCA or Joint Committee decision approving, supporting, objecting to, consenting to, sponsoring or funding a Bristol WPL in the bounded official public-record search.
- A named future package may require MCA assurance or funding approval if it uses an MCA stream, seeks programme entry, requires CRSTS/TCR change control or depends on a regional delivery programme.

Prohibited:

- WECA/MCA has approved, supports, funds or sponsors the Bristol WPL.
- WECA/MCA consent is required or not required.
- WECA/MCA has no role.
- The Bristol WPL is a WECA/MCA scheme.
- CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit or investment-programme assurance is statutory WPL approval.
- Stage 2G public-search absence proves no-role, no-consent or no-funding-dependency status.
- A Bristol Transport Investment Strategy, CAZ context or transport revenue strategy settles WPL net-proceeds accounting or CAZ repayment treatment.

## Sign-Off Criteria

A simulated legal, governance or finance due-diligence agent may give at most **simulation sign-off with conditions** unless all of the following are complete:

1. every WECA/MCA reference is tagged as `strategic context`, `conditional funding/assurance interface`, `formal decision`, `statutory power`, `assumption` or `gap`;
2. every CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit, investment-fund or delivery-assurance reference has a package-level trigger analysis;
3. no document converts strategic alignment into support, silence into no-role or assurance relevance into WPL consent;
4. residual P0s remain visible in gate reporting;
5. any future formal WECA/MCA consultation response, funding decision, assurance decision or legal advice is separately classified by source, decision-maker, date, power or policy basis and effect.

If any dossier text asserts WECA/MCA approval, no-role, consent status, statutory authority or package funding without formal evidence, the simulated review result must be **simulation rework required** or **simulation no-go**.

## Stage 2H Finding

Stage 2H controls package-level WECA/MCA funding and assurance trigger language. It narrows the funding-assurance dependency risk by creating a trigger taxonomy and required package classification. It does not close `RISK-0002` or `ISS-0002`, because WECA/MCA no-role, consent, consultation-response and final funding-dependency conclusions remain unresolved until named packages and cost lines are classified and any formal decisions are evidenced.
