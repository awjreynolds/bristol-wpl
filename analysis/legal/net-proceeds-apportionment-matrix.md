# Net Proceeds Apportionment Matrix

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

Is net-proceeds apportionment required?

## Current Law

`SRC-LEG-0004` includes controls for shares of net proceeds and accounts for schemes involving more than one authority or combined-authority shares where applicable.

## Analysis

No apportionment can be designed until the promoter and WECA/MCA role are settled. If the scheme is Bristol-only and Bristol is the sole relevant authority, apportionment may be unnecessary. If a joint, concurrent, delegated, combined-authority or funding-linked route applies, the programme must define authority shares, spending powers, local transport policy linkages and accounting treatment.

## Uncertainty

WECA/MCA role and any joint-scheme requirement are unresolved P0 controls.

## Scheme Consequence

All financial case revenue tables must be marked provisional until the apportionment question is closed.

## Evidence Required

- Confirmed authority route.
- Legal opinion simulation on net-proceeds recipient and applying authority.
- Funding/assurance dependency map.

## Decision

Hold a placeholder apportionment matrix with no shares assigned.

## Professional Reviewer

Simulated Finance Review Agent, WECA Governance Agent and Legal Review Agent.

## Risk If Unresolved

The scheme may credit, spend or account for proceeds through the wrong body.
