# Legal Authority And Governance Map

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. This is a simulated due-diligence artefact, not legal advice, statutory confirmation, officer approval or professional sign-off.

## Controlled Stage 2B Position

The repo evidence supports a conservative working route for simulation drafting only: a Bristol Workplace Parking Levy would be treated as a Transport Act 2000 workplace parking licensing scheme made by order, with an initial England scheme requiring submission to and confirmation by the Secretary of State as the appropriate national authority before coming into force. Section 184 evidence is held as `SRC-LEG-0003`; section 198 interpretation evidence is held as `SRC-LEG-0020`; the 2009 Regulations RPI-only confirmation exemption is held as `SRC-LEG-0011` and is not treated as available for an initial Bristol order.

Direct WPL section evidence is now acquired in `SRC-LEG-0017` to `SRC-LEG-0020`. Those sources identify licensing-scheme, local-licensing-scheme, joint-local-licensing-scheme and interpretation controls. They reinforce that the authority question is a local traffic authority/licensing authority mapping problem, not a general transport-policy preference.

Stage 2B is recorded in `analysis/legal/stage-2b-current-law-role-map.md`. It narrows the DfT/Secretary of State distinction and keeps Bristol promoter/delegation and WECA/MCA role as open P0 controls for any move beyond legal/governance baseline work.

## Bristol Governance Evidence

The September 2024 decision notice and minutes evidence approval to progress Stage One Development and OBC work at up to GBP 1m, to test options, and to enable a more informed later decision on whether to proceed to FBC and potential delivery. They also evidence CAZ funding treatment and delegated procurement/contract authority for Stage 1 work. They do not evidence any implementation decision, FBC approval, statutory consultation launch, preferred boundary, charging level, employer threshold, exemptions package or statutory submission.

The May 2025, October 2025 and March 2026 acquired decision notices and minutes classify those WPL items as update/information reports that were noted by committee. They do not evidence approval to implement a scheme, launch statutory consultation, approve an OBC/FBC, select a preferred boundary, set charge levels, set employer thresholds, approve exemptions or submit an order for confirmation.

Current Bristol constitution evidence has been acquired. It supports a more precise governance map: Part 2 sets the key-decision and policy-committee framework, Part 3.1 identifies Full Council reserved functions, Part 3.2 gives the Transport and Connectivity Committee transport-policy, transport-maintenance, major-transport-project, local-joint-transport-plan, capital-programme and departmental-risk functions, Part 3.4 sets officer-delegation controls, Part 4.2 sets forward-plan/access-to-information controls, and the Growth and Regeneration officer scheme gives City Transport and related highways/parking/Clean Air Zone operational delegations. None of those sources by itself expressly names the WPL licensing scheme order-making or submission function.

## WECA/MCA Evidence Position

WECA/MCA evidence supports strategic context and possible funding/assurance interfaces only. JLTP4 discusses demand management and workplace-parking-levy-type schemes as possible regional policy tools. WECA bus and BSIP sources reference WPL/demand management as policy/funding context. The assurance framework is relevant if any MCA or Single Pot/CRSTS-funded transport package interface is used. None of those sources is formal WECA/MCA approval, consent, support, objection or statutory-route confirmation for a Bristol WPL.

WECA/MCA constitution material is now acquired in `SRC-WECA-0011` to `SRC-WECA-0013`. It records broad transport, local-transport-plan, transport-operations, funding and decision-procedure material, but it does not expressly decide Bristol WPL approval, consent or no-role status. `SRC-WECA-0007` provides the current assurance framework if MCA funding or programme assurance is engaged. `SRC-WECA-0014` to `SRC-WECA-0016` provide public transport and committee-route context only.

`SRC-LEG-0010` and `SRC-LEG-0014` amend the West of England Combined Authority Order 2017, including omission of Part 3 transport in the extracted source. `SRC-LEG-0022` and `SRC-LEG-0023` confirm the current revised article pages carry that Part 3 omission note. `SRC-LEG-0016` confirms transport and local infrastructure as a strategic-authority area of competence under the 2026 Act. These are current-law mapping inputs, not a completed WPL function conclusion. `SRC-LEG-0015` is explicitly not a WPL power source; it concerns charges for works in maintainable highways and is retained only to avoid future misclassification.

## DfT / Secretary Of State Distinction

DfT pre-application engagement may be used to test dossier expectations, policy evidence expectations, timetable and submission format. It must be classified as informal officer engagement, policy expectation, procedural requirement, formal decision or confirmation material. No DfT engagement may be treated as statutory confirmation unless the formal decision-maker, power and confirmed order are evidenced. Stage 2B reduces the DfT/Secretary of State confusion risk from P0 to a controlled P1, but it does not make the statutory submission ready.
