# Legal Authority And Governance Map

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. This is a simulated due-diligence artefact, not legal advice, statutory confirmation, officer approval or professional sign-off.

## Controlled Stage 2 Position

The repo evidence supports a conservative working route for simulation drafting only: a Bristol Workplace Parking Levy would be treated as a Transport Act 2000 workplace parking licensing scheme made by order, with an initial England scheme requiring submission to and confirmation by the appropriate national authority before coming into force. Section 184 evidence is held as `SRC-LEG-0003`; the 2009 Regulations RPI-only confirmation exemption is held as `SRC-LEG-0011` and is not treated as available for an initial Bristol order.

This baseline does not settle the local promoter, Bristol internal delegation route, WECA/MCA current-law role, or the decision-maker for submission of the order. Those remain P0 controls for any move beyond legal/governance baseline work.

## Bristol Governance Evidence

The September 2024 decision notice and minutes evidence approval to progress Stage One Development and OBC work at up to GBP 1m, to test options, and to enable a more informed later decision on whether to proceed to FBC and potential delivery. They also evidence CAZ funding treatment and delegated procurement/contract authority for Stage 1 work. They do not evidence any implementation decision, FBC approval, statutory consultation launch, preferred boundary, charging level, employer threshold, exemptions package or statutory submission.

The October 2025 and March 2026 acquired materials remain update/information evidence unless a specific later decision notice proves a further approval. The repo has no acquired May 2025 WPL decision/minute evidence.

## WECA/MCA Evidence Position

WECA/MCA evidence supports strategic context and possible funding/assurance interfaces only. JLTP4 discusses demand management and workplace-parking-levy-type schemes as possible regional policy tools. WECA bus and BSIP sources reference WPL/demand management as policy/funding context. The assurance framework is relevant if any MCA or Single Pot/CRSTS-funded transport package interface is used. None of those sources is formal WECA/MCA approval, consent, support, objection or statutory-route confirmation for a Bristol WPL.

`SRC-LEG-0010` amends the West of England Combined Authority Order 2017, including omission of Part 3 transport in the extracted source. That is a current-law trigger, not a completed conclusion. The repo must obtain or verify the consolidated current combined-authority functions, current WECA/MCA constitution/delegations and Bristol constitution before the P0 can close.

## DfT / Secretary Of State Distinction

DfT pre-application engagement may be used to test dossier expectations, policy evidence expectations, timetable and submission format. It must be classified as informal officer engagement, policy expectation, procedural requirement, formal decision or confirmation material. No DfT engagement may be treated as statutory confirmation unless the formal decision-maker, power and confirmed order are evidenced.
