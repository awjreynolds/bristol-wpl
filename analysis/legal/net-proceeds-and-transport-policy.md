# Net Proceeds And Transport Policy

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

How must WPL net proceeds be linked to transport policy?

## Current Law

`SRC-LEG-0004` Schedule 12 evidence requires net proceeds to be applied for transport-policy purposes according to the relevant authority/share route. It requires a 10-year general plan and 5-year detailed programme for relevant schemes made by non-metropolitan local traffic authorities. It also includes provisions for combined authorities where relevant.

## Analysis

The financial case must reconcile:

- gross licence-charge income;
- operating costs;
- enforcement and bad-debt treatment;
- net proceeds;
- statutory account;
- transport-policy linkage;
- delivery programme and benefits logic.

No net-proceeds transport package can be selected at Stage 2. Bristol minutes record committee discussion about showing benefits and potential ringfencing, but that does not select an investment programme.

## Uncertainty

The authority/share route is unresolved because Bristol/WECA role is unresolved. No investment programme is selected.

## Scheme Consequence

The OBC/FBC financial case must not use revenue as general budget support. All proceeds logic must be tied to local transport policy and statutory accounting.

## Evidence Required

- Local transport policy crosswalk.
- 10-year general plan.
- 5-year detailed programme.
- Net-proceeds account design.
- If joint or WECA/MCA route applies, apportionment matrix.

## Decision

Build controls now; do not select programme until options and governance are settled.

## Professional Reviewer

Simulated Finance Review Agent and Legal Review Agent.

## Risk If Unresolved

The business case could overstate affordability or propose unlawful use of net proceeds.
