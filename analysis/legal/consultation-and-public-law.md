# Consultation And Public Law

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

What consultation and public-law controls are required?

## Current Law

The Bristol September 2024 report text in `SRC-BCC-0003` records legal professional comments that consultation must occur at a formative stage, give sufficient reasons, allow sufficient time and be conscientiously taken into account. Transport Act consultation and inquiry provisions remain to be mapped in the statutory matrix.

## Analysis

The consultation must be designed so that the decision-maker has a real choice. The OBC must not predetermine WPL if the evidence supports alternatives or a do-minimum option. Consultation materials must distinguish:

- open questions;
- settled legal constraints;
- provisional assumptions;
- options being compared;
- evidence limitations;
- how responses will be analysed;
- reconsultation triggers.

## Uncertainty

No consultation strategy, stakeholder list, questionnaire, accessibility pack or consultation legal sign-off exists.

## Scheme Consequence

No consultation launch readiness. Stage 8 must remain blocked.

## Evidence Required

- Consultation strategy.
- Consultation compliance table.
- Accessibility review.
- Privacy notice.
- Decision-maker briefing template.
- Reconsultation trigger log.

## Decision

Use consultation principles as design controls only until Stage 8.

## Risk If Unresolved

The decision could be vulnerable to procedural fairness or predetermination challenge.
