# Environmental Assessment Screening

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

What environmental assessment screening is needed?

## Current Law And Evidence

WECA JLTP4 evidence includes environmental assessment and project-level assessment language for major schemes (`SRC-WECA-0001`). The need to screen environment, carbon and assessment issues for this simulation comes from `CODEX_MASTER_PROMPT.md` section 16.

## Analysis

Stage 2 screening inference: a WPL is primarily a charging/licensing and transport-investment mechanism, but funded interventions and traffic displacement could create environmental effects needing later screening. The programme must therefore screen:

- strategic environmental implications of the investment programme;
- air quality and carbon impacts;
- traffic displacement;
- habitats and environmental assessment needs for funded schemes;
- construction impacts if net proceeds fund physical interventions;
- cumulative impacts with CAZ, CRSTS and mass-transit works.

## Uncertainty

No boundary, investment programme, model output or environmental appraisal exists.

## Scheme Consequence

No environmental benefit or no-significant-effect claim can be made.

## Evidence Required

- Environmental screening note.
- Carbon and air-quality model method.
- Funded-intervention screening matrix.
- Reappraisal triggers.

## Decision

Keep environmental assessment as Stage 5/Stage 7/Stage 11 dependency.

## Professional Reviewer

Simulated Environment and Carbon Review Agent.

## Risk If Unresolved

The business case may overstate environmental benefits or miss assessment duties for funded interventions.
