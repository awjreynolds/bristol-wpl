# Scheme Order And Confirmation Process

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

What is the controlled route for making and confirming an initial Bristol WPL order?

## Current Law

`SRC-LEG-0003` states that a licensing scheme under the relevant Part shall not come into force unless the order making it has been submitted to and confirmed by the appropriate national authority, subject to exceptions specified in regulations. `SRC-LEG-0020` identifies the Secretary of State as the appropriate national authority for licensing schemes relating only to England. `SRC-LEG-0011` regulation 3 gives a narrow RPI-only variation exemption.

## Analysis

For simulation control, the order route is:

1. Confirm promoter/order-maker and internal approval route.
2. Prepare scheme order and supporting statutory dossier.
3. Conduct legally adequate consultation before final decision.
4. Consider consultation outcomes conscientiously.
5. Approve final order for submission through the confirmed route.
6. Submit to the appropriate national authority for confirmation.
7. Treat confirmation as a formal Secretary of State decision, not DfT engagement.

## Uncertainty

Final WPL licensing-scheme order-maker and submitter remain P0 open. Stage 2D controls the OBC/progression/FBC member route only. Current DfT expectations are not acquired as a formal procedure source. The DfT/Secretary of State distinction itself is now a controlled P1 route rule under Stage 2B.

## Scheme Consequence

The statutory dossier checklist can be built, but no statutory submission simulation can pass.

## Evidence Required

- Current route confirmation from law/constitution sources.
- DfT pre-application engagement record.
- Clause-by-clause order contents matrix.
- Consultation compliance table.

## Decision

Treat Secretary of State confirmation as required for an initial England order unless specific current-law evidence displaces it. Do not treat informal DfT engagement as confirmation.
