# Charges Thresholds Discounts And Exemptions

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

How should charge levels, thresholds, discounts and exemptions be controlled before scheme design?

## Current Law

The statutory and regulation evidence shows the scheme order and licensing system must control charges, liable parties, vehicle classes, exemptions and penalty charge amounts. `SRC-LEG-0011` supports the RPI-only variation exemption and penalty charge controls. `SRC-LEG-0013` controls vehicle-class categories.

## Analysis

No Bristol charge, threshold, discount or exemption can be treated as selected. The September 2024 decision supports option testing only. Any charging design must be tested through:

- statutory vires and equalities screening;
- business impact and distributional analysis;
- revenue and behavioural modelling;
- consultation design;
- subsidy-control screening where mitigations confer selective economic advantage.

## Uncertainty

No preferred charge level, employer threshold, discount schedule, exemption schedule or vehicle-class policy is evidenced.

## Scheme Consequence

OBC can compare options later, but Stage 2 cannot recommend or lock a charging design.

## Evidence Required

- Options framework and appraisal specification.
- Statutory vehicle-class mapping.
- Equality and subsidy-control screening for each exemption/discount.
- Sensitivity tests for revenue and behavioural response.

## Decision

Keep all charge and exemption settings as assumptions until Stage 5/Stage 7.

## Professional Reviewer

Simulated Legal Review Agent, Equality Review Agent, Subsidy Control Review Agent and Economic Appraisal Review Agent.

## Risk If Unresolved

The scheme may be irrational, discriminatory, unsupported by consultation or financially unreliable.
