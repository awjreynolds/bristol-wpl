# Equality Human Rights And Proportionality

Status: Stage 2 technical baseline. Simulation only; not legal advice or professional sign-off.

## Question

What equality, human rights and proportionality controls apply before scheme selection?

## Current Law And Evidence

Bristol EqIA sources are acquired for September 2024, September/October 2025 and related packs (`SRC-BCC-0007`, `SRC-BCC-0010`, `SRC-BCC-0027`). Stage 1 reviews did not treat these as final scheme EqIAs. The requirement for equality, health and distributional appraisal across options comes from `CODEX_MASTER_PROMPT.md` sections 15 and 24.

## Analysis

Stage 2 screening inference: a WPL could affect employers, employees and people whose travel choices or parking needs differ by disability, shift pattern, caring responsibility, income, study status or local transport accessibility. This is a risk-screening hypothesis for future EqIA and distributional analysis, not an evidenced Bristol impact finding. Equality and human-rights work must be option-sensitive, not a final-stage add-on.

## Uncertainty

No final boundary, charge, threshold, exemptions, mitigation package or consultation evidence exists.

## Scheme Consequence

No equality-safe preferred option can be recommended yet.

## Evidence Required

- Updated EqIA for each shortlist option.
- Human-rights/proportionality note.
- Distributional model outputs.
- Consultation response analysis by protected characteristic where lawful and proportionate.

## Decision

Keep equality/human-rights as Stage 5, Stage 7, Stage 8 and Stage 11 gates.

## Professional Reviewer

Simulated Equality Review Agent and Legal Review Agent.

## Risk If Unresolved

The decision could be unfair, disproportionate or unsupported by equality evidence.
