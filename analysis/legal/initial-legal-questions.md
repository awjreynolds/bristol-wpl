# Initial Legal Questions

Status: working simulation issue list. This is not legal advice.

1. Is the simulated proposal an initial WPL scheme order, a variation, a revocation or another statutory action?
2. For an initial order, what confirmation route applies under current Transport Act 2000 provisions?
3. Which body is the competent licensing authority for the simulated Bristol scheme area?
4. Are WPL functions held by Bristol City Council, West of England MCA/WECA, or concurrently/delegated under current instruments?
5. Can the simulated scheme cover part of Bristol only, and what boundary/legal schedule constraints follow?
6. What statutory and public-law consultation duties apply before order-making and confirmation?
7. What Schedule 12 and Net Proceeds Regulations accounting controls apply?
8. What HRA, equality, data-protection, subsidy-control and environmental-screening issues are decision-critical?

## P0 Controls From D1 Discovery

- Confirm Bristol City Council status and delegation route as WPL licensing authority/order-maker.
- Confirm whether current devolution/WECA/MCA law changes alter WPL functions, concurrence, submission or governance.
- Confirm who submits the initial order for Secretary of State/appropriate national authority confirmation.
- Confirm current DfT/Secretary of State non-statutory evidence expectations without treating engagement as approval.

## P1 Controls From D1 Discovery

- Stage 2K partially controls revocation and variation terminology; complete publication, objections, notice, DfT process, public-law consultation trigger and Bristol decision/signatory route mapping before closure.
- Settle consultation, inquiry, exemptions/discounts, enforcement, data, equality, subsidy/procurement and environmental-law implications.
