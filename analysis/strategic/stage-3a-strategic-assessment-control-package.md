## Control Position

Stage 3A defines the control framework for problem definition, baseline and trends, objectives, critical success factors, theory of change, benefits, dependencies and investment-programme classification. It does not determine that a Workplace Parking Levy is the preferred intervention.

The strategic assessment tests whether a WPL, non-WPL intervention, investment-only package, or combined package could address the evidenced problems.

Stage 3A does not:

- select a preferred scheme;
- select a WPL-funded transport package;
- approve any project or cost line;
- quantify benefits;
- set targets;
- claim WECA/MCA approval, funding, consent, sponsorship or no-role status;
- unblock OBC/FBC assembly, consultation or statutory submission.

Stage 3A does not claim WECA/MCA approval, funding, consent, sponsorship or no-role status.

## Strategic Problem Control

Future problem definition must distinguish:

- observed Bristol evidence;
- policy aspiration;
- comparator evidence;
- forecast/modelled evidence;
- stakeholder claim;
- assumption or gap.

No Stage 3A problem statement may assert that WPL is the only solution. It may test whether WPL logically addresses a problem only after baseline evidence and alternative options are visible.

Each future problem statement must cover transport, accessibility, environmental, fiscal and equity dimensions separately, or mark the missing dimension as a gap.

## Objectives and CSFs Control

Objectives must be SMART where evidence allows and must not prejudge WPL. They must be capable of supporting alternative interventions, including non-WPL demand management, parking management, public transport, active travel, planning/parking standards, investment-only and combined packages.

Critical success factors must include:

- strategic fit;
- legal feasibility;
- transport effectiveness;
- value for money;
- affordability;
- revenue resilience;
- deliverability;
- operational enforceability;
- equality and social acceptability;
- cross-boundary coherence;
- consultation defensibility;
- environmental performance;
- adaptability.

Objectives must be outcome-based and intervention-neutral. Revenue can be a fiscal constraint or funding mechanism; it must not be framed as the strategic objective that predetermines WPL.

## Theory of Change Control

The theory of change must separate:

1. WPL charge and licensing effects;
2. employer and employee behavioural responses;
3. funded transport package effects;
4. combined package effects;
5. disbenefits and failure mechanisms;
6. monitoring and evaluation indicators.

It must expose tensions including revenue maximisation versus traffic reduction, scheme success reducing the charge base, charging before alternatives are available, Bristol-only action in a regional labour market, boundary leakage, displacement, business competitiveness and investment-package dependence.

Future theory-of-change rows must classify each causal pathway as WPL-only, transport-investment-only or combined-package, and must identify failure mechanisms including behavioural erosion, parking displacement, business impacts, inclusion impacts, boundary leakage and revenue/investment dependency.

## Stakeholder Control

Future stakeholder assessment must identify affected groups and places without inventing consultation responses, support or opposition. Entries must distinguish statutory consultees, affected employers, workers, disabled people, low-income groups, neighbouring authorities, WECA/MCA, DfT and delivery partners.

## Investment Programme Control

No package or cost line may be used in an OBC/FBC claim until it records:

- stable package or cost-line ID;
- sponsor;
- objective;
- scope;
- maturity;
- approvals;
- cost and inflation basis;
- funding sources;
- dependency on WPL;
- operating cost;
- delivery timetable;
- benefits;
- interaction with other projects;
- consent;
- procurement;
- risk;
- contingency;
- business-case status;
- ability to scale or defer if revenue underperforms;
- WECA/MCA trigger classification and gate effect.

Stage 3A creates package controls only. It does not select any package.

Until a candidate package or cost line has funding source, MCA assurance trigger, OAR/ASR/ASST route, reviewer and gate effect recorded, its only safe status is candidate/interface under review.

## WECA/MCA and Funding Assurance Control

Every future package or cost line must apply the Stage 2H trigger taxonomy before OBC/FBC reliance. CRSTS, TCR, mass-transit, investment-fund and delivery-assurance material remains conditional package-level funding/assurance interface evidence only unless a formal decision source proves a stronger role.

WECA/MCA assurance may become relevant if a named package or cost line uses, matches, changes, depends on, or interfaces with MCA funding, assurance, delegated approvals, or regional delivery arrangements.

If MCA funding, MCA assurance, CRSTS, Single Pot, TCR/LTDP, mass transit, programme-entry, delivery-funding or programme-change control is triggered without a formal decision route, package reliance is P0-blocked until a formal decision source and assurance route are recorded.

Do not claim:

- WECA/MCA approval, funding, sponsorship or support exists for a WPL package;
- WECA/MCA consent is required or not required;
- WECA/MCA has no role;
- a WPL-funded package has MCA assurance approval;
- a package funding stack is secured;
- a package benefit is attributable to WPL before appraisal separates WPL-only, investment-only and combined effects.

## Benefits Control

No benefit, target, BCR contribution, VFM effect, mode shift, congestion reduction, distributional effect or investment benefit may be claimed until the benefits registers are populated with objective link, measure, baseline, target, attribution route, source evidence, uncertainty, owner, reviewer and status.

Each future benefit must classify whether it is WPL-only, transport-investment-only or combined-package. Gross levy receipts must be treated as a transfer/public cash-flow item, not an economic benefit.

## Stage 3A Acceptance Criteria

Stage 3A can be treated as complete for control purposes only when:

- required strategic and investment-programme control files exist;
- `strategic-qa` passes;
- package and cost-line classification templates exist;
- objectives and CSFs explicitly avoid prejudging WPL;
- benefits and dependencies are labelled as unquantified/control placeholders;
- simulation sign-off records that Stage 3, OBC/FBC and consultation readiness remain blocked.
