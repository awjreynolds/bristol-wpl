Key Decision No

Purpose of Report

To consider changes to schemes within the Investment Fund programme against
the agreed delivery assurance principles.

Recommendation

The West of England Combined Authority Committee:

1) Approves the change requests for schemes within the current Investment
Fund programme as set out in Appendix 1.

2) Agrees the flexibility to allow the West of England Combined Authority, Bath
& North East Somerset Council, Bristol City Council and South
Gloucestershire Council to move CRSTS risk budgets between CRSTS
projects within their respective CRSTS programmes, subject to the criteria
set out in paragraphs 5 and 6 in this report.

Reasons for recommendation

Change requests outside previously agreed tolerances for delegated decisions
require Committee approval.

It is recommended that the Committee approve an amendment to the CRSTS
programme’s change control tolerances, reflecting the programme’s transition into
delivery and the continuing development of cost management maturity across the
region.
Voting arrangements

In order to be carried, a decision on this matter requires a majority of the members
present and voting. Such majority is to include the Mayor. Each member present
may cast one vote. If a vote is tied the decision is not carried. There is no casting
vote. Co-opted members are not entitled to vote.

Publication Requirements

For publication.

Background

1. At the West of England Mayoral Combined Authority (MCA) Committee meeting
in July 2022, a set of delivery assurance and budgetary principles were agreed to
drive the delivery of projects and good practice, one of which was the Portfolio
Review Board (PRB) comprising the West of England MCA and Unitary Authority
S73/S151 officers and Directors. An updated Terms of Reference of the PRB were
agreed by the West of England MCA Committee in January 2024.

2. To support timely decision making, in September 2022 a set of Investment Fund
delegations to manage change requests were granted to the MCA Director of
Infrastructure in consultation with the Directors of Infrastructure of the Unitary
Authorities for schemes within the infrastructure portfolio. In October 2023, the
MCA granted equivalent delegations to the Director of Environment in consultation
with the Directors of Environment of the relevant Unitary Authorities for change
requests for schemes within the Green Recovery Fund programme.

3. At the MCA Committee in July 2025, the authority to approve change requests
within approved budgets, for schemes in the transport portfolio within the
Investment Fund and CRSTS programmes, was delegated to the Executive
Director for Transport of the West of England Combined Authority in consultation
with the West of England Combined Authority S73 Officer, Infrastructure Directors
and S151 Officers from the Unitary Authorities.

4. At the MCA Committee in March 2026, the authority to approve all CRSTS change
requests was delegated to the West of England Combined Authority’s Executive
Director for Transport in consultation with the West of England Combined
Authority S73 Officer, equivalent Directors from the Unitary Authorities, and S151
Officers.
Key Considerations

CRSTS Risk Budgets
5. The CRSTS Programme is carrying out a lesson learned review. As part of this
review the Programme held a discussion with the CRSTS Board where it was
recommended that further flexibility be provided for schemes that are in delivery.
The Board agreed to proceed with providing flexibility to move risk funding
between CRSTS only projects that already have full business cases approved and
delivery funding awarded. This flexibility would apply only to projects with an
approved final business case and where there is no change to agreed scope or
outcomes, no impact on local contribution commitments, and continued
compliance with the agreed delivery milestones of contractual commitment by
March 2027 and completion by March 2029.

6. Each organisation would be required to submit a change request to the CRSTS
Board on a quarterly basis that provides a summary of any movements of risk and
contingency budgets between projects. The change request would include details
about any consequent impacts on drawdowns, their timing, and spend profiles,
supported by the appropriate audit trail. The change requests will be for noting by
the CRSTS Board and will then be published as an officer decision to support the
grant funding mechanism.

7. The Programme is currently working alongside the colleagues from the PMO,
Finance, Grant Assurance and Delivery Leads across the four organisations to
ensure that the risk budgets for each project are clear, and to finalise the detailed
guidance and conditions for this process. A detailed proposal will then be
presented for approval at the CRSTS board to enable the process to be
implemented.

Delivery Assurance
8. In spring 2025, a CRSTS Programme Board was established to provide oversight
and assurance of the totality of CRSTS programme investment, and collectively
hold responsibility for monitoring portfolio progress and resolving issues that may
compromise delivery and benefits realisation. This brings together the MCA and
UA transport Directors and S151/S73 officers, and in respect of CRSTS scheme
changes this effectively functions as the PRB. On this basis, a number of change
requests have been recommended for approval through the CRSTS Programme
Board and approved through previously agreed delegations.

9. In April 2026, a Joint Regional Directors Forum (JRDF) was established with the
aim to provide a regular opportunity to support decision making, information
sharing and development of proposals relevant to the West of England Combined
Authority. The membership of this Forum includes the Combined Authority and
UA Directors. The Terms of Refence of the JRDF include consideration of Change
Requests recommended for approval at the MCA or Joint Committee and as such
the JRDF will act as the PRB going forward.

10. In line with the agreed process, the JRDF considered a number of change
requests on 7 May 2026. A total of 32 projects have submitted change requests

Appendix 1. As requested at the Overview and Scrutiny Committee meeting in

Key Risks

17. Project business cases set out the way in which risk will be managed and include
a risk register identifying key risks to project delivery. These business cases are
published at the point of approval by the West of England MCA.

18. All projects underway maintain a specific risk register as part of the project
management and monitoring arrangements. Key risks are included in regular
Highlight Reports.

Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Implications

19. In developing the proposals set out in this paper, consideration has been given
to the Public Sector Equality Duty under the Equality Act 2010. This report
primarily relates to delivery assurance, reprofiling and change requests for
schemes within the current programme. The equality implications therefore sit
mainly in how changes to scope, timing or delivery may affect when and how
different groups experience the intended benefits of each scheme. Where
projects support active travel, public realm, housing, regeneration, green space,
climate resilience, skills, digital access or employment outcomes, teams should
continue to consider accessibility, inclusive design, safety, community
engagement and the potential for delayed benefits to affect some communities
more than others. Where any change request materially alters scope, delivery
approach or intended outcomes, the relevant equality analysis or EqIA should be
reviewed and updated to ensure ongoing compliance with the Public Sector
Equality Duty.

Health Improvement and Health Inequalities
20. The promoting organisation will be responsible for ensuring that any health
implications are considered through the relevant project governance and
management processes in enacting the proposed project changes set out in
Appendix 1 of this report.

Climate Change Implications

21. The MCA has committed to addressing the climate and ecological emergencies
and reiterated these commitments through proposals in the regional growth
strategy.

22. The Green Growth West Fund (GGWF) was set up as an impact fund for the
region, to support regional net zero infrastructure and SME growth. The closure
of the fund and the return of the capital and revenue funding means that the
positive outcomes proposed for climate change will no longer be met through this
route.

23. The other change requests relating to climate and nature projects are seeking
changes to timelines – the impact will be a delay, but still delivering the same
outcomes. The exception is Managing More Greenspace for Nature, which should
achieve additional positive outcomes through utilising underspend from the
project.

Financial Implications

24. The change requests would increase Investment Fund headroom by £753k,
reflecting £523k returned from the Green Growth West Fund and £230k from FWD
Phase 0. A further £817k underspend from the Mayoral Priority Skills Fund would
be returned to the Employment and Skills Fund allocation.

25. The remaining change requests do not increase approved funding but amend
spend profiles, milestones, cost categories or scope within existing allocations.
Reprofiled spend must continue to be managed within approved budgets, funding
conditions and the overall Investment Fund programme.

26. The proposed CRSTS risk budget flexibility does not increase the overall CRSTS
allocation. Any movements between approved CRSTS projects must remain
within each organisation’s programme envelope, maintain agreed scope and
outcomes, and be supported by appropriate change control, audit trail and
financial monitoring.

Legal Implications

27. Each approved Change to a project will need to comply with any provisions for
change contained within the relevant legal documentation. Legal Services will
provide Project Teams with legal support in relation to each approved Change to
ensure compliance with legal and governance requirements.

Human Resources Implications

28. If the changes are approved and for situations that result in increased funding with
the need for more staff resource than currently exists, HR will work with the
relevant officer/lead to ensure the request to recruit approvals and CLT approvals
are in place, followed by the Combined Authority’s recruitment or agency process
to appoint all posts – including fixed term, permanent, interim, agency or
contractor.

29. In situations where funding is reduced, the Combined Authority’s internal
managing change and redeployment processes will be applied where applicable.
Commercial and Procurement

30. If the changes outlined in the report result in amendments to any existing
contracts, these will be subject to procurement regulations and will require the
appropriate level of commercial governance, including approval from the
Commercial Board. Further engagement with the Commercial team will be
necessary to assess the implications in more detail.
