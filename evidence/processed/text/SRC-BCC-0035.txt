(a) Subject: Policy Decision on Cycleway Legibility
(agenda item 9)

(b) Ward:
(c) Declarations of interest:
None

(d) Decision taken

The Transport and Connectivity Committee approved a trial of red coloured cycleways at
suggested options of high-conflict points, such as Old Market Gap and Bristol Bridge
(with an incremental approach to St Augustine’s Parade when appropriate) in order to
inform a further policy decision on cycleway surfacing. As subject to funding, the final
selection was delegated to the Executive Director Growth & Regeneration in consultation
with the Chair of the Transport & Connectivity Policy Committee.

(a) Subject: City Centre – Broadmead Area/ Union Street Area Outline Business
Case and delivery (agenda item 13)

(b) Ward:
(c) Declarations of interest:
None

(d) Decision taken

That the Committee for Transport & Connectivity;
1. Approved to submit the Broadmead/Union St Area Outline Business Case (OBC) to
the West of England Combined Authority (WECA) which, if approved, would secure
funding to continue development of the project up to Full Business Case (FBC)
stage.
2. Authorised the Executive Director for Growth and Regeneration in consultation
with Chair of the Transport and Connectivity Committee to take all steps required
to accept and spend the funding, and to procure and award the necessary
contracts, or professional services for this project up to FBC (which may be over the
key decision threshold), in-line with the procurement routes and maximum budget
envelopes outlined in this report, should the OBC be approved by WECA. This will
include costs to secure vacant possession of two kiosks at the north end of Union
St.
3. Delegated authority to approve the subsequent FBC to the Executive Director for
Growth and Regeneration in consultation with Chair of the Transport and
Connectivity Committee.
4. Authorised the Executive Director for Growth and Regeneration in consultation
with Chair of the Transport and Connectivity Committee to take all steps required
accept and spend the funding, and to procure and award the necessary contracts,
or professional services for this project delivery (which may be over the key
decision threshold), in-line with the procurement routes and maximum budget
envelopes outlined in this report, should the FBC be approved by WECA.
