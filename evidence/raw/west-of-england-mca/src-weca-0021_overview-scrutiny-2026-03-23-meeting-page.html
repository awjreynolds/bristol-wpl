1.

Mayor's Q&A

Minutes:

The Chair welcomed  Mayor Helen Godwin to the meeting for a Question and Answer session.   Members had been invited to submit up to two questions with the opportunity for two follow-up questions: 

 

1. Clean Energy: The Mayor identified clean energy as one of the region’s key growth sectors and noted the presence of 400 specialised companies in the area that were focused on this.  She highlighted the importance of supporting those businesses to grow.  She mentioned the ‘Warm Homes Plan’ which Government had chosen to launch in the West of England and also regional ambitions for solar, tidal energy and offshore wind.  She considered that with the current situation globally the government had been very robust about the commitment to renewables and energy security and she saw this as the way forward.

2. Locally Generated Income Tax: The Mayor welcomed government proposals for Combined Authorities to retain portions of income tax. She considered that this would provide the fiscal autonomy needed to address the transport network, which she characterised as the primary barrier to regional growth.  She welcomed the Overnight Visitor Levy which would also enable the region to consider the specific transport challenges in relation to the visitor economy, nighttime economy and people working in those sectors.  She commented that if the region were to get an Integrated Settlement then that would enable us also to think about how we organise our own spending so that we can support transport more robustly.  The MCA was also expecting a second TCR (Transport for City Regions) in the next spending review as well.

3. Avonmouth & Severnside Infrastructure: Members challenged the Mayor on the ‘lived experience’ of workers in these industrial areas, citing dangerous walking routes, lack of lighting, and roaming dogs. The Mayor acknowledged that infrastructure planning had previously been too vehicle-centric and committed to working with community groups on localised safety improvements. 

4. Congestion Management: Members questioned the Mayor on her stance regarding congestion charging. The Mayor emphasised that residents must have a viable public transport alternative before such measures were introduced. She noted that current roadworks to improve the infrastructure were ‘painful’ but necessary for long-term improvement. She committed to improve the buses and highlighted the significant work around rail and the commitment to mass transit in order to give people other options, including micro mobility.  

5. MCA’s Business Plan: Members asked the Mayor to consider her top three priorities. She explained that the MCA was now focussed on delivery and said that firstly Transport was the key thing that she considered was holding the region back, so getting that right was really important both in the immediate sense but also in that longer term planning.  Secondly, gaining Established Status because that would unlock more funding and unlocked the Integrated Settlement which would enable the region to be a lot more ambitious.  Thirdly, ensuring that through our Skills Strategy we were creating opportunities not just for young people, but working age adults and people who were economically inactive  ...  view the full minutes text for item 1.

WRITTEN PUBLIC QUESTIONS (written procedure)

 

1. Any member of the public can submit a maximum of two written questions in advance of this meeting.

 

2. The deadline for the submission of questions is 5.00 pm, at least 3 clear working days ahead of a meeting. For this meeting, the deadline for questions is 5.00 pm on 17 March 2026.

 

3. Questions should be addressed to the Chair of the meeting and e-mailed to democratic.services@westofengland-ca.gov.uk

 

4. Under the direction of the Chair, wherever possible, written replies to questions will be sent to questioners by the end of the working day prior to the meeting.

 

5. Please note - under the Combined Authority’s committee procedures, there is no opportunity for oral supplementary questions to be asked at committee meetings.

 

6. The written questions and replies will be circulated to committee members in advance of the meeting and published on the Combined Authority website.

 

PUBLIC STATEMENTS

 

1. Any member of the public may submit a written statement (or petition) to this meeting.

 

2. Please note that one statement per individual is permitted.

 

3. Statements must be submitted in writing and received by the deadline of 12 noon on the working day before the meeting. For this meeting, the deadline for statements is 12 noon on 20 March 2026. Statements should be emailed to democratic.services@westofengland-ca.gov.uk

 

4. Statements will be listed for the meeting in the order of receipt. All statements will be sent to committee members in advance of the meeting and published on the Combined Authority website.

 

5. Please note:

 

If any member of the public wishes to attend the meeting to orally present their statement, they are asked please to notify the Combined Authority’s Democratic Services team of this at the point when their statement is submitted and by 12 noon on the working day before the meeting at the very latest.

 

For those presenting their statements at the meeting, up to 3 minutes ‘speaking time’ is permitted for each statement. The total time available for the public session at this meeting is 30 minutes.

Digital and IT Systems Strategy Overview PDF 117 KB

To consider the report on the Digital and Data Strategy

Additional documents:

  • 0.8a Appendix 1 Digital and Data Strategy Executive Summary , item 7. PDF 76 KB

Minutes:

The Head of Digital and Data joined the meeting to introduce this report which provided the Committee with an update on the development of the MCA's Digital and Data strategy.  He said that it focussed on strengthening the Authority's internal digital and data capabilities, including the systems, data governance platforms and workforce skills that supported how the organisation operated. This was a key work stream within the Authority's Organisational Improvement Programme. The Authority currently delivered its digital and data services through a combination of internal capability and partnerships, which included a partnership with North Somerset Council and DELT. Through the development of the strategy, the team had identified that the Authority had a number of valuable data sources and examples of good practice.  The opportunity now was to better connect that data and treat it as a corporate asset so that it could be used more consistently to support decision reporting and insight across the organisation.

 

The following was noted from discussions:

 

-        Members asked about the ethical use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the Authority.  The Officer explained that it formed part of the Digital Foundations theme and that there would be an initiative to develop clear guidance and policy on ethical use of AI as part of the strategy.

-        Members asked about Open Data.  The Officer confirmed that in both the Trusted and Connected data theme which was about our digital foundations, the Authority were looking at how the organisation could better connect and reuse data. Also in work to develop the roadmap, the team were  looking at how to develop the data architecture in a safe and compliant way so that both internal users and external users could trust the data that they were seeing and understand this.

-        Members asked for details of what  systems were currently in place and a stronger sense of what IT systems would be needed over the next few years.

 

Resolved:

That the report be noted.
