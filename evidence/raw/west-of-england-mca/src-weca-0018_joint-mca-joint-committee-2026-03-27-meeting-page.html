Chair's Announcements

To receive any announcements from the Chair of the West of England Combined Authority Committee.

Minutes:

The following announcements were made by Mayor Godwin:

 

The Chair welcomed members and highlighted a wide range of activity since the previous meeting. A public consultation on North Somerset joining the Combined Authority was live until 10 April 2026, and residents and businesses were encouraged to respond.

 

The Chair reported on recent regional initiatives, including the establishment of a new Business Board, chaired by Jo Dally and Nick Sturge, and the successful hosting of the British Business Bank’s first Investor Roadshow in Bristol, attended by over 170 investors. Her visits across the region included the BBC Natural History Unit, Wake the Tiger, and the new Youth Zone in South Bristol.

 

A newly discovered species of club fungus in Bath and North East Somerset had drawn national and international attention, and members were encouraged to volunteer for the West of England Wildlife Index Project ahead of the forthcoming State of Nature Report.

 

On transport matters, the Chair highlighted the publication of a new joined?up transport vision, record government investment, and continued progress on improving bus, rail, walking, and cycling infrastructure. A new agreement with the Department for Transport would secure half?hourly rail services across key routes. Engagement sessions had begun in advance of major works on the Portishead Line, which would significantly reduce commuting times for over 50,000 residents. Work on delivering five new rail stations continued, including progress on Bristol Brabazon Station adjacent to the Aviva Arena.

 

The West Innovation Arc had entered national consultation regarding possible designation as a new town, supported by the government. The region had secured £45 million from the Brownfield Housing Fund—its first allocation—aimed at unlocking new homes, including affordable housing. The Chair also marked the start of work on the Jingle Jam House, the UK’s first patient hotel for families of children undergoing hospital treatment.

 

The Chair announced the launch of a new Voluntary and Community Sector Support Fund, part of the Regional Youth Guarantee Trailblazer, with applications recently closed.

 

On buses, child fares had been frozen at £1 until spring 2029, free travel schemes have been extended for care leavers and youth guarantee participants, and the Kids Go Free initiative will return for the Easter holidays, having already doubled journey numbers in several lower?income areas.

 

The Chair invited leaders to update the committee on their respective advisory boards:

 

  • Planning, Housing and Regeneration Advisory Board (Cllr Dyer): Positive first meeting covering terms of reference, new development proposals, homelessness and rough sleeping, cladding remediation, and a new regional affordable housing portal.
  • Transport Advisory Board (Cllr Guy) Constructive inaugural meeting focused on the strategic transport plan, governance improvements, data?driven monitoring, capacity challenges, and plans for topic?specific workshops.
  • Economy and Skills Advisory Board (Cllr Tyrrell): Collaborative first meeting addressing skills funding changes, community learning, school engagement, priority sectors, and development of a regional skills strategy. Consideration will be given to increasing meeting frequency.

 

No update was available for the Climate and Nature Advisory Board.

Comments from the West of England Combined Authority Overview & Scrutiny Committee PDF 47 KB

To receive comments from the Chair (or Chair’s representative) on behalf of the Combined Authority’s Overview & Scrutiny Committee.

Minutes:

Councillor Jerome Thomas, Chair of the Authority’s Overview & Scrutiny Committee, attended the meeting and addressed the Committee with the comments received from that Committee. The Overview & Scrutiny Committee’s comments had been published and circulated prior to the meeting and are summarised as follows:

 

Cllr Thomas began by expressing concern regarding the recent incident that occurred at the meeting of the Authority’s Overview & Scrutiny Committee on the previous  Monday and confirmed his Committee’s support for the additional safety measures introduced.

 

Business Plan

  • The Overview & Scrutiny Committee had noted the extensive number of actions within the Business Plan.
  • The Mayor had been asked to identify her top three priorities for the year, which had been confirmed as:
    1. Transport
    2. Gaining established status
    3. Delivering the skills strategy
  • The Committee had welcomed the introduction of the Social Value Framework.

Transport Vision

  • Scrutiny members had met with the Mayor and the Director of Transport in advance of the meeting to discuss the Transport Vision; this was welcomed.
  • However, the Committee believed scrutiny input should have been sought prior to the vision being finalised and publicised.
  • Given the high public interest, the Committee highlighted several points for the Combined Authority Committee to note:
    • Congestion: The need for clear short- and medium?term measures to tackle congestion, acknowledging that mass transit was a long-term solution. These should include both incentives and deterrents (“carrot and stick”).
    • Funding: Recognition that current funding was inadequate to meet transport ambitions, and central government contributions would only partly close the gap.
    • Congestion Charging: The Mayor had advised that any consideration of congestion charging rested with Bristol and Bath & North East Somerset councils.
    • Design Principles: The Committee would have liked to see stronger emphasis on health, sustainability and safety within the vision, and recommended these be integrated in the next planning stages.
    • Engagement: Greater public and stakeholder engagement was essential, particularly in the context of budget constraints.
    • Bus Network Control: The Committee supported increased ambition for bus network control, including a staged progression toward franchising and full public ownership, with clear timelines and scheduled scrutiny involvement.

Transport for City Region (TCR)

  • The Committee welcomed the increase in budget from £10m to £50m within the TCR proposal, enabled through an increased overprogramming rate of 25%.
  • While supportive of investment in active travel, the Committee cautioned that overprogramming could embed a risk of underperformance.
  • The Committee emphasised the need for strong public engagement on mass transit priorities, network design, and sequencing.

Joint Working

  • The Committee welcomed improved coordination between the Director of Transport and the Directors of the Unitary Authorities, recognising this as a positive step toward streamlining decision?making.

Other Points

  • The Committee welcomed the new glossary of projects within the budget papers for aiding clarity.
  • The Committee expressed disappointment at the loss of the £10m Green Growth West Fund but welcomed the creation of the £25m Creative Place Growth Fund and were reassured about the planned distribution measures.

Social Value in Procurement and Contracts PDF 130 KB

This paper formalises the West of England Mayoral Combined Authority’s (MCA) approach to Social Value in Procurements and Contracts.

Additional documents:

  • Item 11A - Social Value in Procurement App 1 , item 11. PDF 3 MB
  • Item 11B - Social Value in Procurement App 2 , item 11. PDF 508 KB

Decision:

Committee noted the proposed approach to Social Value in Procurement and Contracts.

Minutes:

This paper formalised the West of England Mayoral Combined Authority’s (MCA) approach to Social Value in Procurements and Contracts by embedding Social Value principles in the Authority’s procurement and contracts.

 

The Mayor, in introducing this item, stated that the updated Social Value Framework focused on three themes that were core to what the Authority was doing:

 

  • Family and homes, which meant reducing child poverty, supporting children’s early years development, and strengthening early intervention in areas like homelessness;
  • Economy and growth, through supporting people into work and reducing barriers to opportunity, and increasing VCSE and SME participation in supply chains;
  • And nature and climate, which meant taking meaningful action to reduce pollution and support nature recovery, and creating more green communities.

 

The Mayor stated that she believed that by embedding social value across spending mechanisms proportionately, which would include all above-threshold procurements, the Authority would be able to focus its efforts where its impact could be the greatest. It would also help us to foster effective, long-term relationships with the VCSE sector and delivery partners.

 

The proposals were endorsed by the other leaders who felt this would be an important lever and tool for the Authority and indicated their consent for the proposal.

 

Resolved:

 

Committee noted the proposed approach to Social Value in Procurement and Contracts.

[Joint Committee item]

 

Following the draft National Planning Policy Framework (NPPF) that was published for consultation by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG) in December 2025, this paper sought to further define the scope of the Spatial Development Strategy (SDS).

 

The Mayor stated in her introduction to this item that the SDS would be a comprehensive plan to guide how the West of England would grow in the future. Following consultation with government, the Authority wanted to make sure that the SDS had progressed by the end of the current Parliament, and this paper would be important in determining the core scope of future work and funding.

 

The SDS was an opportunity to plan how the coming decades would unfold, as the region worked to deliver on the ambitions outlined in the Growth Strategy. To support this crucial work, the Authority had secured a grant of some £1.6 million. This work would also factor in how to protect the region’s landscapes and biodiversity, restore nature, and strengthen its climate resilience.

 

Cllr Dyer welcomed the progress made in relation to a regional development plan and would help to forecast future behaviour and therefore affordable housing and transport links and by looking beyond local authority boundaries. 

 

Cllr Guy supported the proposals and stated that it was important that the SDS supported and added value to local plans that were either already in existence or were being worked upon.  He stated that while connections between Bath and Bristol were vital, with Saltford Station a vital component, it was also important to recognise the rural nature of parts of the region and preservation of green corridors would be vital any SDS.

 

In response Mayor Godwin stated that it was important to encourage these rural areas to flourish.

 

Cllr Tyrrell welcomed the work that was being done and said that each area was at a different stage with their local plans with South Gloucestershire just about to enter examination in public stage.  She felt that the work that had already been carried out by the UAs had been recognised and could be pulled together for a regional plan.  The long term vision would be important as the region moved through the plan over the next few years and she looked forward to the further work that would be carried out.

 

Cllr Gibbons echoed the earlier comments and stated that as this would be a plan for the next 10, 20, 30 years it was important to engage with young people regarding its proposals.  She welcomed the collaboration on this policy.

 

The recommendations were moved by Cllr Gibbons and seconded by Cllr Tyrrell.
