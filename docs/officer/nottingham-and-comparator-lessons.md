# Nottingham And Comparator Lessons

Status: officer-facing lessons control.  
Date: 2026-06-26.

This document records lessons only. It does not claim that Nottingham outcomes will happen in Bristol.

## Core Lesson

Nottingham is useful because it shows what an implemented WPL can require in practice: governance, employer support, licensing operations, parking controls, transport investment, monitoring and long-term public explanation.

The Bristol simulation must not copy Nottingham charge levels, elasticity, mode shift, congestion effects, parking-base assumptions or employer responses without Bristol evidence.

Stage 18A turns this into an operational lesson-control layer. It still does not claim that Nottingham outcomes will happen in Bristol, and it does not make CPZ/RPZ mitigation, displacement modelling, consultation material or a boundary option ready.

## Transferability Questions For Bristol

| Transferability question | Why it matters for Bristol | Evidence route | Bristol transfer condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Potential residential spillover must be assessed early | Commuters may avoid workplace charges by parking on nearby streets | `SRC-BCC-0006`; `SRC-UK-0003`; Stage 4 displacement controls | Boundary options, residential street baseline, CPZ/RPZ or other mitigation options to be appraised, designed, costed and consulted on before reliance |
| Residential baseline comes before boundary reliance | Without street-level evidence, a boundary can shift pressure to residents and create avoidable consultation risk | `NLR-0007`; `analysis/economic/nottingham-displacement-control-checklist.csv` | Parking occupancy baseline, employment-area stress mapping and affected-street evidence |
| CPZ/RPZ mitigation is not automatically ready | Parking controls can mitigate spillover but require design, equality, cost, enforcement and consultation evidence | `NLR-0008`; `PIT-0005` | Mitigation option map, enforcement capacity, equality screen, consultation route and decision authority |
| Monitoring needs triggers before it is credible | A monitoring promise is weak unless thresholds, data owners and response routes are defined | `NLR-0009` | Baseline metrics, trigger thresholds, response governance and public reporting route |
| Public transport package matters | WPL is not only a charge; it depends on credible alternatives and reinvestment | `SRC-NOTT-0002`; `SRC-ACADEMIC-0002`; `SRC-UK-0003` | Bristol package, funding route, delivery maturity and benefits attribution |
| Employer support and onboarding matter | Licensing compliance depends on employers understanding liability and options | `SRC-NOTT-0001`; `SRC-NOTT-0002`; `SRC-BCC-0006` | Bristol employer inventory, guidance, shadow/testing period and appeals process |
| Nottingham charge or threshold cannot be copied | Bristol boundary, employer mix, affordability and transport alternatives differ | `analysis/economic/nottingham-transferability-matrix.csv` | Bristol-specific appraisal and consultation evidence |
| Reported mode shift is not automatic causation | Behavioural evidence is mixed with transport investment and wider trends | `SRC-ACADEMIC-0002`; `SRC-ACADEMIC-0001` acquisition gap | Bristol model, baseline and sensitivity testing |
| Non-implementation lessons matter | Leicester/TfL material highlights proposal-stage concerns and displaced parking controls | `SRC-UK-0003`; `SRC-TFL-0001` | Bristol consultation, CPZ/RPZ, equality and business-impact evidence |
| Current Nottingham evidence must be refreshed | Older reports do not prove current charge, threshold or operating status | `EG-0008`; `NLR-0010` | Latest official Nottingham pages, annual reporting, charge/threshold source notes and currentness review |

These are transferability questions, not Bristol forecasts. Learning from Nottingham does not mean Bristol would get the same impacts, and it does not mean Bristol already has a parking-mitigation plan.
