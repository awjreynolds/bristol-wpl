# Legal And Governance Briefing

Status: officer-facing simulation briefing.  
Date: 2026-06-26.

This is not legal advice. It translates current simulation controls into a human-readable route map.

## Current Legal Route Position

The simulation uses the Transport Act 2000 WPL licensing-scheme framework as the statutory spine. An initial England-only scheme is controlled as requiring Secretary of State confirmation before coming into force.

That does not mean Bristol can proceed. The final order-maker, statutory submitter and signatory route remain a P0 blocker.

## Bristol Route

Current controls support Bristol-led authority-status investigation and a Transport and Connectivity Committee route for OBC progression and FBC decisions. They do not close the final scheme-order route.

Before reliance, a future route note must identify:

- the body making the WPL licensing-scheme order;
- whether Full Council, committee or another route is required;
- the officer or member signatory;
- the statutory submitter to the Secretary of State;
- Monitoring Officer and legal basis for the route.

## WECA/MCA Route

WECA/MCA evidence is currently controlled as strategic context and possible funding or assurance interface evidence only.

The simulation does not conclude that WECA/MCA approves, consents, objects, has no role, has a transferred WPL function or has no funding dependency.

## DfT And Secretary Of State

DfT engagement is not the same as Secretary of State confirmation. Generic DfT business-case and TAG guidance may shape the OBC/FBC and appraisal approach, but it does not prove a WPL-specific confirmation dossier route.

## Legal Do-Not-Say List

- Bristol has approved implementation.
- Bristol final order-making is settled.
- Officer-only final order-making is available.
- WECA/MCA has approved, objected, consented or has no role.
- DfT has accepted the dossier or confirmed the process.
- Consultation is legally safe to launch.
- A statutory submission is ready.
