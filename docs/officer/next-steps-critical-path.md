# Next Steps Critical Path

Status: officer-facing handover control.
Date: 2026-06-26.

## What happens next

This page converts the live no-go position into work packages. The critical path is not approval. It is a practical map of what would need to be evidenced, reviewed and decided before any real Bristol WPL approval, consultation, OBC, FBC or statutory submission could be considered.

No work package closes a blocker by existing. Each package is blocked until source evidence, professional review and the relevant gate checks are complete.

## Critical Path

1. Resolve authority and role questions: Bristol route, WECA/MCA role and DfT process.
2. Build spatial and operational evidence: boundary, parking inventory, displacement, DPIA and service design.
3. Build appraisal and finance evidence: OAR, ASR, ASST, model outputs, uncertainty, net proceeds and Section 151 review.
4. Prepare consultation only after authority, privacy, accessibility, equality, material and analysis controls are ready.
5. Assemble OBC/FBC evidence only after upstream dependencies exist and review panels can test claims.
6. Keep public and officer material aligned with the no-go registers.

## No-Go Position

- The 90-day plan does not authorise spend or procurement.
- The work packages do not approve a WPL or close any blocker.
- The handover does not replace officer, legal, finance, DfT, WECA/MCA, consultation, data, modelling or democratic decisions.
- All WPL gates remain blocked until their own evidence and validator checks pass.
